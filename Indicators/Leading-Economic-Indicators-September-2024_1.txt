Leading Economic IndicatorsKEY HIGHLIGHTS
The Monthly Leading Economic Indicators report highlights changes in Consumer Price Indices,
inflation rates, interest rates and exchange rates. In addition, the report details changes in se-
lected indicators of international trade, agriculture, energy, manufacturing, construction, tourism
and transport.
MACROECONOMIC INDICATORS
The Consumer Price Index (CPI) increased from an index of 139.87 in August 2024 to 140.13 in
September 2024, as shown in Table 1(a). The overall year-on-year inflation rate, as measured by
the CPI, was 3.6 per cent in September 2024, as presented in Table 1(b).
The Kenyan Shilling appreciated against the US Dollar and Tanzanian Shilling but depreciated
against the Sterling Pound, Euro, Japanese Yen, South African Rand, and Ugandan Shilling in
September 2024, as shown in Table 2. The average yield rate for 91-day Treasury Bills de-
creased from 15.85 per cent in August 2024 to 15.75 per cent in September 2024. The Central
Bank of Kenya maintained its benchmark interest rate at 12.75 per cent in September 2024. The
interbank rate declined from 13.01 per cent in August 2024 to 12.67 per cent in September
2024. The weighted average lending rate at commercial banks rose from 16.84 per cent to 16.91
per cent, while the weighted average deposit rate declined from 11.59 per cent to 11.24 per
cent. Overdraft rates increased from 16.39 per cent to 16.83 per cent, while the savings rate
dropped from 4.62 per cent to 3.57 per cent during the same period, as detailed in Table 3.
The NSE 20 Share Index increased from 1,678 points in August 2024 to 1,776 points in Septem-
ber 2024. However, the total number of shares traded decreased from 392 million to 334 million.
The number of equity transactions rose from 25,560 to 27,733, while equity turnover fell from
KSh 6.5 billion to KSh 5.0 billion during the same period, as detailed in Table 4.
The money supply (M1) increased from KSh 1.9 trillion in August 2024 to KSh 2.0 trillion in Sep-
tember 2024. Quasi Money decreased from KSh 2.8 trillion in August 2024 to KSh 2.7 trillion in
September 2024. Consequently, the money supply (M2) fell slightly to KSh 4.7 trillion in Septem-
ber 2024. The money supply (M3) contracted by 0.30 per cent to KSh 6.0 trillion in September
2024, as shown in Table 5(a). Gross Foreign Exchange Reserves grew by 2.3 per cent to KSh 2.1
trillion in September 2024, while Net Foreign Exchange Liabilities rose from KSh 884.2 billion in
August 2024 to KSh 895.8 billion in September 2024, as indicated in Table 5(b).
Pa ge | 1AGRICULTURE
The quantity of coffee auctioned at the
Nairobi Coffee Exchange decreased from
3.0 thousand metric tonnes (MT) in August
2024 to 2.6 thousand MT in September
2024, while the price rose from USD 4.80
per kilogram (kg) to USD 4.91 per kg dur-
ing the same period, as shown in Table 6.
Tea production declined from 40.8 thousand MT in July 2024 to 36.6 thousand MT in August
2024. Similarly, tea auction volume decreased from 34.1 thousand MT in July 2024 to 25.1 thou-
sand MT in August 2024. However, the average auction price rose from USD 2.21 per kg in July
2024 to USD 2.26 per kg in August 2024. For the first eight months of 2024, the average auction
price was USD 2.21 per kg, as shown in Table 7.
Cane deliveries declined from 800.3 thousand MT in August 2024 to 796.9 thousand MT in Sep-
tember 2024, as shown in Table 8. Tables 9(b) and 9(d) present the average retail market prices
for one kg of maize and one kg of beans, respectively. The average monthly retail market price
for one kg of maize fell from KSh 48.2 in August 2024 to KSh 48.0 in September 2024. Similarly,
the average monthly retail market price for one kg of beans decreased from KSh 150.2 to KSh
147.6 during the same period.
The quantity of coffee exported decreased from 5.7 thousand MT in August 2024 to 5.0 thousand
MT in September 2024, with the export value falling from KSh 3.7 billion to KSh 3.4 billion. The
quantity of tea exported rose from 50.8 thousand MT in August 2024 to 51.9 thousand MT in
September 2024, while its value fell from KSh 14.7 billion to KSh 14.4 billion, as detailed in Table
10.
The volume of exported cut flowers increased from 6.3 thousand MT in August 2024 to 8.6 thou-
sand MT in September 2024, with their value rising from KSh 4.9 billion to KSh 6.2 billion. Simi-
larly, the volume of exported vegetables rose from 6.8 thousand MT to 9.0 thousand MT, with
their value increasing from KSh 2.1 billion to KSh 2.6 billion. In contrast, the volume of exported
fruits decreased from 18.1 thousand MT to 15.9 thousand MT, accompanied by a decline in value
from KSh 4.6 billion to KSh 3.2 billion during the same period, as outlined in Table 11.
Pa ge | 2INTERNATIONAL TRADE AND ICT
The total volume of trade decreased from
KSh 319.9 billion in August 2024 to KSh
310.4 billion in September 2024. The ex-
port value dropped from KSh 93.4 billion
to KSh 91.0 billion, while the import value
declined from KSh 226.5 billion to KSh
219.4 billion during the same period, as
shown in Table 12.
In September 2024, Uganda and the USA were Kenya's primary export destinations, with export
values of KSh 10.8 billion and KSh 6.1 billion, respectively, as outlined in Table 13(a). The domi-
nant export category was food and beverages, which accounted for 44.0 per cent of total ex-
ports. Non-food industrial supplies comprised 24.7 per cent, while machinery and other capital
equipment constituted 2.0 per cent of total exports, as presented in Table 13(b).
China and the United Arab Emirates were the leading importing partners in September 2024,
with import values of KSh 53.4 billion and KSh 29.3 billion, respectively, as outlined in Table 14
(a). The predominant import category was non-food industrial supplies, which accounted for 35.2
per cent of total imports. Fuel and lubricants made up 21.4 per cent, machinery and other capital
equipment constituted 14.6 per cent, and food and beverages accounted for 11.6 per cent of to-
tal imports, as shown in Table 14(b).
The number of active mobile money agents increased from 348,065 in August 2024 to 367,551 in
September 2024, while mobile money subscriptions rose from 78.6 million to 79.4 million. How-
ever, the total number of transactions fell from 214.3 million to 196.5 million, and the total value
of transactions declined from KSh 705.9 billion to KSh 670.5 billion during the same period, as
outlined in Table 14(c). The exports value of telecommunications equipment increased from KSh
119.3 million in August 2024 to KSh 364.1 million in September 2024. Similarly, the imports value
rose from KSh 2.7 billion to KSh 2.9 billion. Notably, the exports value of recording equipment
surged from KSh 0.1 million to KSh 129.2 million, while the exports value of automatic data pro-
cessing machines and storage units grew from KSh 32.4 million to KSh 47.0 million during the
same period, as detailed in Table 14(d).
Pa ge | 3ENERGY, CONSTRUCTION AND MANUFACTURING
Local electricity generation decreased from
1,096.2 million KWh in August 2024 to 1,045.5
million KWh in September 2024, as shown in
Table 15(a). Total electricity generation and
imports fell from 1,227.3 million KWh in August
2024 to 1,167.9 million KWh in September
2024. KPLC's total sales declined from 923.1
million KWh to 905.4 million KWh, and electricity imports fell from 131.1 million KWh to 122.4
million KWh. In contrast, electricity exports rose from 2.7 million KWh to 3.7 million KWh during
the same period, as detailed in Table 15(b).
In September 2024, the national average retail prices for motor gasoline premium, light diesel oil,
and illuminating kerosene were KSh 189.46, KSh 172.33, and KSh 159.10 per litre, respectively.
Charcoal prices averaged KSh 85.32 per kg, and a 13-kg gas cylinder had an average price of
KSh 3,196.04 during the same period, as detailed in Table 15(e). On the international market,
OPEC crude oil basket prices decreased from USD 78.41 per barrel in August 2024 to USD 73.59
per barrel in September 2024, as shown in Table 15(f).
The value of approved residential building plans by Nairobi City County increased from KSh 15.7
billion in August 2024 to KSh 25.1 billion in September 2024. In contrast, the value of approved
non-residential building plans decreased from KSh 2.5 billion to KSh 2.2 billion during the same
period, as outlined in Table 16.
Sugar production rose from 73.4 thousand MT in August 2024 to 73.8 thousand MT in September
2024, as detailed in Table 17(a). Soft drink production declined from 64.5 million litres in August
2024 to 59.5 million litres in September 2024, as outlined in Table 17(b). Production of assem-
bled vehicles increased from 1,007 units in August 2024 to 1,061 units in September 2024, as
presented in Table 17(c). Production of galvanized sheets fell from 25.0 thousand MT in August
2024 to 21.8 thousand MT in September 2024, as presented in Table 17(d). Cement production
decreased from 780.6 thousand MT in August 2024 to 728.5 thousand MT in September 2024.
Similarly, cement consumption declined from 763.7 thousand MT to 713.1 thousand MT during
the same period, as shown in Table 17(e). Milk intake in the formal sector rose from 74.2 million
litres in August 2024 to 75.5 million litres in September 2024, as indicated in Table 17(f).
Pa ge | 4TOURISM AND TRANSPORT
The total number of visitor arrivals at Jomo
Kenyatta International Airport (JKIA) and
Moi International Airport (MIA) fell from
175,113 in August 2024 to 144,996 in Sep-
tember 2024. MIA recorded a 22.5 per cent
drop, decreasing from 19,822 to 15,371,
while JKIA reported a 16.5 per cent decline,
falling from 155,291 to 129,625 during the same period, as shown in Table 18.
Tables 19(a) and 19(b) present embarked passengers by port of destination and landed passen-
gers by port of origin through JKIA, respectively. The number of embarked passengers by port of
destination rose from 259,304 in May 2024 to 283,104 in June 2024. Similarly, the number of
passengers who landed by port of origin increased from 257,876 to 296,807 during the same pe-
riod. The total number of newly registered motor vehicles and motorcycles, categorized by body
type, fell from 13,499 units in July 2024 to 9,888 units in August 2024. The number of newly reg-
istered motorcycles dropped from 6,060 units to 4,292 units during the same period, as shown in
Table 19(c).
The number of passengers traveling by the Standard Gauge Railway (SGR) declined from 281,683
in August 2024 to 175,901 in September 2024, resulting in a corresponding drop in revenue from
KSh 440.6 million to KSh 296.4 million. Similarly, the volume of cargo transported via the SGR
decreased from 582.4 thousand tonnes to 512.5 thousand tonnes, in contrast, revenue from car-
go transport slightly increased to KSh 1.2 billion during the same period, as shown in Table 19
(d). Metre Gauge Railway (MGR) passenger usage declined from 240,026 persons in August 2024
to 231,684 persons in September 2024. Revenue from MGR passenger traffic decreased from KSh
13.7 million to KSh 12.4 million during the same period, as indicated in Table 19(e).
Table 19(f) presents Mombasa Port throughput data. The total volume of cargo handled de-
creased from 3.5 million MT in August 2024 to 3.4 million MT in September 2024. Export volume
fell from 410.8 thousand MT to 410.0 thousand MT, while import volume declined from 2.7 mil-
lion MT to 2.5 million MT. In contrast, transshipment volume grew from 380.2 thousand MT to
493.6 thousand MT, and restows increased from 8.8 thousand MT to 14.4 thousand MT, during
the same period.
