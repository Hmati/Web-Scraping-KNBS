iiKEY HIGHLIGHTS
The Monthly Leading Economic Indicators report highlights changes in Consumer Price Indices,
inflation rates, interest rates and exchange rates. In addition, the report details changes in se-
lected indicators of international trade, agriculture, energy, manufacturing, construction, tourism
and transport.
MACROECONOMIC INDICATORS
The Consumer Price Index (CPI) increased from an index of 138.40 in April 2024 to an index of
139.64 in May 2024, as indicated in Table 1(a). The overall year on year inflation rate as meas-
ured by the Consumer Price Index (CPI) was 5.0 per cent, in May 2024, as presented in Table 1
(b).
The Kenyan Shilling appreciated against the Japanese Yen and the Tanzanian Shilling, but depre-
ciated against the US Dollar, Sterling Pound, Euro, South African Rand, and Ugandan Shilling in
May 2024, as outlined in Table 2. The average yield rate for 91-day Treasury Bills, which is a
benchmark for interest rate trends, dropped from 16.16 per cent in April 2024 to 15.91 per cent
in May 2024. The Central Bank of Kenya maintained its benchmark interest rate at 13.0 in May
2024. Interbank rates dropped from 13.70 per cent in April 2024 to 13.62 per cent in May 2024,
as presented in Table 3.
The NSE 20 Share index rose from 1,691 points in April 2024 to 1,722 points in May 2024. The
total volume of shares traded increased from 301 million to 510 million. The total number of eq-
uity transactions increased from 27,190 to 27,334 during the same period. The Equities Market
turnover more than doubled to KSh 16.0 billion in May 2024 compared to KSh 7.3 billion record-
ed in April 2024, as outlined in Table 4.
Money supply M2 decreased from KSh 4,585.2 billion in March 2024 to KSh 4,464.5 billion in
April 2024. Broad money supply (M3) declined by 1.09 per cent, from KSh 5,900.6 billion in
March 2024 to KSh 5,836.3 billion in April 2024. Foreign currency deposits increased from KSh
1,315.4 billion to KSh 1,371.8 billion during the same period, as shown in Tables 5(a). The value
of Gross Foreign Exchange Reserves increased from KSh 2,031.8 billion in March 2024 to KSh
2,096.5 billion in April 2024. Net Foreign Exchange Liabilities increased from KSh 752.0 billion to
KSh 824.2 billion during the same period, as outlined in Tables 5(b).
3AGRICULTURE
The quantity of coffee auctioned at
the Nairobi Coffee Exchange rose
from 4.8 thousand MT in February
2024 to 5.1 thousand MT in March
2024. The price of coffee auctioned
dropped from US Dollars 5.09 per Kg
to US Dollars 4.84 per Kg, during the
same period, as presented in Table 6.
Tea production decreased from 55.4 thousand MT in February 2024, to 54.3 thousand MT in
March 2024. The average auction price for Kenya tea was lower at US Dollars 2.15 per Kg in
March 2024 compared to US Dollars 2.23 per Kg in February 2024, as shown in Table 7.
Cane deliveries rose by 10.4 per cent from 756.0 thousand MT in February 2024 to 834.9 thou-
sand MT in March 2024, as shown in Table 8. Tables 9(b) and 9(d) present the average retail
market prices for one Kg of maize and one Kg of beans, respectively. The average monthly retail
market price for one Kg of maize decreased from KSh 54.4 in April 2024 to KSh 52.3 in May
2024. Similarly, the average monthly retail market price for one Kg of beans dropped from KSh
154.1 to KSh 149.2, during the same period.
The quantity of exported coffee decreased from 5.4 thousand MT in April 2024, to 4.7 thousand
MT in May 2024, with a corresponding decrease in value from KSh 3.9 billion to KSh 3.6 billion,
during the same period. The quantity of exported tea decreased from 56.3 thousand MT in April
2024 to 51.6 thousand MT in May 2024. In addition, its value dropped from KSh 17.3 billion to
KSh 15.6 billion during the same period, as outlined in Table 10.
The volume of exported cut flowers fell from 16.1 thousand MT in February 2024 to 8.7 thousand
MT in March 2024, along with a decrease in value from KSh 13.3 billion to KSh 6.1 billion. The
volume of exported fruits more than doubled, rising from 5.0 thousand MT in February 2024 to
12.3 thousand MT in March 2024, with their value increasing from KSh 1.0 billion to KSh 2.0 bil-
lion. The volume of exported vegetables dropped from 16.2 thousand MT in February 2024 to
14.6 thousand MT in March 2024, with a corresponding decline in value from KSh 3.6 billion to
KSh 2.8 billion, as shown in Table 11.
4INTERNATIONAL TRADE AND ICT
Table 12 presents monthly statistics on
external trade. The volume of trade
decreased from KSh 329.1 billion in
April 2024 to KSh 307.1 billion in May
2024. The total export value rose from
KSh 92.9 billion to KSh 94.5 billion dur-
ing the same period. The total import
value decreased from KSh 236.2 bil-
lion in April 2024 to KSh 212.6 billion in May 2024.
In May 2024, Uganda and Pakistan were Kenya's primary export destinations, with values of KSh
10.6 billion and KSh 6.7 billion, respectively, as outlined in Table 13(a). Food and beverages con-
stituted the largest category of exports, accounting for 44.2 per cent, while non-food industrial
supplies made up 26.1 per cent of the total exports, as presented in Table 13(b).
China and the United Arab Emirates were the leading importing partners with values of KSh 47.0
billion and KSh 36.9 billion respectively, in May 2024, as outlined in Table 14(a). The dominant
import category was non-food industrial supplies, constituting 33.8 per cent of the total imports.
Fuel and lubricants accounted for 22.8 per cent of the total imports, while food and beverages
accounted for 10.4 per cent of the total imports during the same period, as shown in Table 14
(b).
The number of mobile money agents increased from 320.3 thousand in March 2024 to 331.6
thousand in April 2024. The number of mobile money subscriptions dropped from 77.7 million to
77.4 million. In addition, the total number of transactions dropped from 207.8 million to 203.6
million, during the same period. The total value of transactions decreased from KSh 747.7 billion
in March 2024 to KSh 711.7 billion in April 2024, as shown in Table 14(c). The export value of
telecommunication equipment increased from KSh 78.8 million in April 2024 to KSh 88.8 million
in May 2024, while the import value decreased from KSh 4.9 billion to KSh 3.7 billion during the
same period, as detailed in Table 14(d).
5ENERGY, CONSTRUCTION AND MANUFACTURING
Local electricity generation rose from
997.8 million KWh in April 2024 to 1,023.5
million KWh in May 2024. Geo-thermal was
the leading source of electricity generation
in May 2024, contributing 43.0 per cent of
the total electricity generated in May 2024.
Wind generation grew by 15.0 per cent to 95.4 million KWh in May 2024 from 83.0 million KWh
in April 2024, as detailed in Table 15(a). Total electricity generation and import grew from
1,126.7 million KWh in April 2024 to 1,152.4 million KWh in May 2024. Total sales by KPLC went
up from 883.0 million KWh to 892.4 million KWh. Electricity imports totaled 128.9 million KWh in
May 2024, while export rose from 3.5 million KWh in April 2024 to 3.9 million KWh in May 2024,
as shown in Table 15(b).
The national average retail prices for motor gasoline premium, light diesel, and illuminating kero-
sene stood at KSh 193.46, KSh 179.91, and KSh 169.52 per litre, respectively, in May 2024. Char-
coal prices averaged KSh 82.32 per kg, while a 13-kg cylinder of gas averaged KSh 3,221.29,
during the same period, as shown in Table 15(e). On the international market, the OPEC crude
oil basket price decreased from US Dollars 89.12 per barrel in April 2024 to US Dollars 83.59 per
barrel in May 2024, as presented in Table 15(f).
The total value of buildings approved by Nairobi City County increased from KSh 16.4 billion in
April 2024 to KSh 23.4 billion in May 2024. The value of approved residential buildings rose from
KSh 13.8 billion to KSh 20.0 billion. Similarly, the value of approved non-residential buildings rose
from KSh 2.6 billion to KSh 3.4 billion during the same period, as outlined in Table 16.
Sugar production increased by 10.2 per cent in March 2024, reaching 69.5 thousand MT, up from
63.1 thousand MT in February 2024, as indicated in Table 17(a). Cement production decreased
from 711.7 thousand tonnes in February 2024 to 706.9 thousand tonnes in March 2024, while
cement consumption rose from 653.4 tonnes to 653.7 thousand tonnes during the same period,
as detailed in Table 17(e). Milk intakes in the formal sector increased from 66.9 million litres in
April 2024 to 76.4 million litres in May 2024, as shown in Table 17(f).
6TOURISM AND TRANSPORT
The total number of arrivals
through both Jomo Kenyatta
(JKIA) and Moi International Air-
ports (MIA) dropped from 146,442
persons in February 2024 to
128,057 persons in March 2024, as
shown in Table 18.
Tables 19(a) and 19(b) present embarked passengers by port of destination and by port of origin,
through Jomo Kenyatta International Airport, respectively. The number of embarked passengers
by port of destination rose from 256,525 in February 2024 to 266,893 in March 2024. The num-
ber of landed passengers by port of origin dropped from 253,717 to 251,616 during the same pe-
riod. The total number of newly registered motor vehicles, categorized by body type, declined
from 18,509 units in February 2024 to 15,984 units in March 2024. Notably, the number of newly
registered motorcycles dropped from 8,561 units to 6,509 units during the same period, as
shown in Table 19(c).
The number of passengers traveling by the Standard Gauge Railway (SGR) rose from 158,652 in
February 2024 to 180,645 in March 2024, with a corresponding revenue increase from KSh 270.4
million to KSh 298.0 million. Cargo volume transported via SGR rose from 342.9 thousand tonnes
to 449.2 thousand tonnes, with a drop in revenue from KSh 981.1 million to KSh 976.1 million
during the same period, as presented in Table 19(d). Metre Gauge Railway (MGR) passenger us-
age rose from 240,623 in February 2024 to 242,976 in March 2024. In addition, revenue generat-
ed from MGR passenger traffic rose from KSh 14.8 million to KSh 15.5 million during the same
period, as presented in Table 19(e).
Table 19(f) presents Mombasa Port throughput. The volume of cargo handled grew from 3.4 mil-
lion MT in April 2024 to 3.6 million MT in May 2024. Import volume dropped from 2,706.2 thou-
sand MT in April 2024 to 2,651.8 thousand MT in May 2024. Export volume rose from 360.3 thou-
sand MT in April 2024 to 423.9 thousand MT in May 2024. Transshipment rose to 366.8 thousand
MT in April 2024 to 468.8 thousand MT in May 2024. Restows more than doubled to 19.3 thou-
sand MT in May from 9.4 thousand MT recorded in April 2024.
