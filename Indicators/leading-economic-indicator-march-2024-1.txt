iiKEY HIGHLIGHTS
The Monthly Leading Economic Indicators report highlights changes in Consumer Price Indices,
inflation rates, interest rates and exchange rates. In addition, the report details changes in se-
lected indicators of international trade, agriculture, energy, manufacturing, construction, tourism
and transport.
MACROECONOMIC INDICATORS
The Consumer Price Index (CPI) increased by 0.2 per cent, from an index of 138.34 in February
2024 to an index of 138.66 in March 2024, as indicated in Table 1(a). The overall year on year
inflation rate as measured by the Consumer Price Index (CPI) was 5.7 per cent, in March 2024,
as presented in Table 1(b).
The Kenyan Shilling appreciated against all major selected trading currencies in March 2024, as
outlined in Table 2. The average yield rate for 91-day Treasury Bills, which is a benchmark for
interest rate trends, rose from 16.51 per cent in February 2024 to 16.68 per cent in March 2024.
The Central Bank of Kenya maintained its benchmark interest rate at 13.0 in March 2024. Inter-
bank rates dropped from 13.59 per cent in February 2024 to 13.42 per cent in March 2024, as
presented in Table 3.
The Nairobi Securities Exchange (NSE) 20 Share index soared by 14.1 per cent from 1,536 points
in February 2024 to 1,752 points in March 2024. Concurrently, the total volume of shares traded
more than doubled, rising from 276 million to 670 million during the same period. The total num-
ber of equity transactions rose from 20,275 in February 2024 to 25,008 in March 2024. Notably,
Equities Market turnover rose to KSh 11.8 billion in March 2024, compared to KSh 4.6 billion rec-
orded in February 2024, as detailed in Table 4.
Tables 5(a) and 5(b) present statistics on Money Supply. Broad money supply (M3) declined by
1.71 per cent, from KSh 6,111.1 billion in January 2024 to KSh 6,006.4 billion in February 2024.
Similarly, foreign currency deposits decreased from KSh 1,603.2 billion to KSh 1,504.1 billion dur-
ing the same period. The value of Gross Foreign Exchange Reserves decreased from KSh 2,431.0
billion in January 2024 to KSh 2,391.6 billion in February 2024. Net Foreign Exchange Liabilities
increased from KSh 857.8 billion to KSh 886.5 billion during the same period.
3AGRICULTURE
The quantity of coffee auctioned at
the Nairobi Coffee Exchange rose
from 4,824.0 metric tonnes in Feb-
ruary 2024 to 5,070.0 metric tonnes
in March 2024. In contrast, its price
dropped from US Dollars 5.09 per
Kg to US Dollars 4.84 per Kg, during
the same period, as presented in Table 6. Tea production decreased from 58,966.9 MT in Janu-
ary 2024, to 55,447.3 MT in February 2024. The average auction price for Kenya tea was higher
at 2.23 USD per Kilogram in February 2024 against 2.19 USD per Kilogram in January 2024, as
shown in Table 7.
Cane deliveries experienced a notable increase of 10.4 per cent, rising from 756.0 thousand met-
ric tonnes in February 2024 to 834.9 thousand metric tonnes in March 2024, as shown in Table 8.
Tables 9(b) and 9(d) presents the average retail market prices for one Kg of maize and one Kg of
beans, respectively. The average monthly retail market price for one Kg of maize decreased from
KSh 61.6 in February 2024 to KSh 57.0 in March 2024. Similarly, the average monthly retail mar-
ket price for one Kg of beans dropped from KSh 159.6 to KSh 156.8 during the same period.
The quantity of exported coffee increased from 2,745.2 MT in February 2024 to 4,291.3 MT in
March 2024, with a corresponding increase in value from KSh 2.1 billion to KSh 3.3 billion, during
the same period. The quantity of exported tea increased from 59,043.7 MT in February 2024 to
59,599.2 MT in March 2024. Conversely, its value dropped from KSh 20.4 billion to KSh 19.1 bil-
lion during the same period, as outlined in Table 10.
Table 11 provides data on the monthly exports of fresh horticultural produce. The volume of ex-
ported cut flowers decreased from 12,841.9 MT in November 2023 to 4,684.6 MT in December
2023, accompanied by a corresponding decrease in value from KSh 9.5 billion to KSh 3.2 billion.
Similarly, the volume of exported fruits declined from 3,474.9 MT in November 2023 to 1,699.5
MT in December 2023, with a decrease in value from KSh 0.6 billion to KSh 0.2 billion during the
same period. Additionally, the volume of exported vegetables decreased from 14,599.8 MT in No-
vember 2023 to 4,777.6 MT in December 2023, with a corresponding decline in value from KSh
7.5 billion to KSh 1.4 billion.
4INTERNATIONAL TRADE AND ICT
Table 12 presents monthly statistics on ex-
ternal trade. The volume of trade declined
from KSh 333.8 billion in February 2024 to
KSh 306.2 billion in March 2024. The total
export value decreased from KSh 107.4
billion to KSh 97.9 billion during the same
period. Additionally, the total import value
decreased from KSh 226.4 billion to KSh
208.2 billion during the same period.
In March 2024, Uganda and Pakistan were Kenya's primary export destinations, with values of
KSh 9.3 billion and KSh 7.6 billion, respectively, as outlined in Table 13(a). Food and beverages
constituted the largest category of exports, accounting for 47.4 per cent, while non-food industri-
al supplies made up 23.2 per cent of the total exports, as indicated in Table 13(b).
Table 14(a) outlines the main origins of imports. China and United Arab Emirates were the lead-
ing importing partners with values of KSh 36.0 billion and KSh 21.6 billion, respectively in March
2024. The dominant import category was non-food industrial supplies, constituting 34.8 per cent
of the total imports. Fuel and lubricants accounted for 24.6 per cent of the total imports, while
food and beverages accounted for 14.3 per cent of the total imports during the same period, as
shown in Table 14(b).
The number of mobile money agents decreased from 321,340 in January 2024 to 320,182 in
February 2024. The number of mobile money subscriptions increased from 76.8 million to 77.3
million. In addition, the total number of transactions grew from 211.6 million to 213.3 million,
during the same period. The total value of transactions rose from KSh 784.0 billion in January
2024 to KSh 790.8 billion in February 2024, as outlined in Table 14(c). The import value of tele-
communication equipment dropped from KSh 3.6 billion in February 2024 to KSh 2.4 billion in
March 2024. Conversely, their export value rose from KSh 0.02 billion to KSh 0.08 billion, during
the same period, as detailed in Table 14(d).
5ENERGY, CONSTRUCTION AND MANUFACTURING
Tables 15(a) and 15(b) presents monthly elec-
tricity statistics. Local electricity generation
rose from 979.0 million KWh in February 2024
to 1,052.0 million KWh in March 2024. Geo-
thermal was the leading source of electricity
generation in March 2024, accounting for
442.7 million KWh. Notably, wind generation rose from 156.1 million KWh in February 2024 to
198.7 million KWh in March 2024. Total electricity generation and imports increased from 1,110.4
million KWh in February 2024 to 1,180.4 million KWh in March 2024. Additionally, total sales by
KPLC went up from 854.0 million KWh to 878.9 million KWh, while total electricity imports de-
creased from 131.5 million KWh to 128.4 million KWh, during the same period.
The national average retail prices for motor gasoline premium, light diesel, and illuminating kero-
sene stood at KSh 199.77, KSh 191.11, and KSh 189.48 per litre, respectively, in March 2024.
Charcoal prices averaged KSh 78.64 per kg, while a 13-kg cylinder of gas averaged KSh
3,231.84, during the same period, as shown in Table 15(e). On the international market, the
OPEC crude oil basket price increased from US Dollars 81.23 per barrel in February 2024 to US
Dollars 84.22 per barrel in March 2024, as presented in Table 15(f).
The total value of buildings approved by Nairobi City County declined from KSh 16.2 billion in
February 2024 to KSh 13.6 billion in March 2024. Specifically, the value of approved residential
buildings decreased from KSh 12.6 billion in February 2024 to KSh 11.6 billion in March 2024.
Similarly, the value of approved non-residential buildings dropped from KSh 3.6 billion to KSh 2.0
billion during the same period, as outlined in Table 16.
Sugar production amounted to 69,520 MT in March 2024, marking a 10.2 per cent increase from
the 63,075 MT recorded in February 2024, as presented in Table 17(a). Cement production de-
creased from 813,372 MT in November 2023 to 758,055 MT in December 2023. Similarly, cement
consumption decreased from 786,042 MT to 724,307 MT during the same period, as outlined in
Table 17(e). Milk intakes in the formal sector declined from 71.8 million litres in February 2024 to
70.3 million litres in March 2024, as outlined in Table 17(f).
6TOURISM AND TRANSPORT
The total number of arrivals through
both Jomo Kenyatta (JKIA) and Moi
International Airports (MIA) dropped
from 154,283 persons in December
2023 to 134,665 persons in January
2024, as indicated in Table 18.
Tables 19(a) and 19(b) presents embarked passengers by port of destination and by port of
origin, through Jomo Kenyatta International Airport. The number of embarked passengers by port
of destination rose from 256,525 in February 2024 to 266,893 in March 2024. Conversely, the
number of embarked passengers by port of origin dropped from 253,717 to 251,616, during the
same period. The number of newly registered motor vehicles, classified by body type, declined
from 12,217 units in November 2023 to 10,349 units in December 2023, as presented in Table 19
(c).
The number of passengers traveling by the Standard Gauge Railway (SGR) rose from 158,652 in
February 2024 to 180,645 in March 2024, with a corresponding revenue increase from KSh 270.4
million to KSh 298.0 million. Cargo volume transported via SGR rose from 342,867 tonnes to
449,200 tonnes, with a rise in revenue from KSh 981.1 million to KSh 976.1 million during the
same period, as presented in Table 19(d). Metre Gauge Railway (MGR) passenger usage rose
from 240,623 in February 2024 to 242,976 in March 2024. In additions, revenue generated from
MGR passenger traffic rose from KSh 14.8 million to KSh 15.5 million during the same period, as
presented in Table 19(e).
Table 19(f) presents Mombasa Port throughput. The volume of cargo handled grew from 2,741.9
thousand tonnes in February 2024 to 3,336.2 thousand tonnes in March 2024. Import volume
rose from 1,877.6 thousand tonnes in February 2024 to 2,375.8 thousand tonnes in March 2024.
Additionally, export volume rose from 371.4 thousand tonnes to 494.2 thousand tonnes. Trans-
shipment saw a modest uptick of 0.1 per cent to 457.7 thousand tonnes, while restows recorded
a significant drop of 76.2 per cent to 8.5 thousand tonnes from 35.7 thousand tonnes, during the
same period.
