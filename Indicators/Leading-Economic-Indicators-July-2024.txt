Leading Economic IndicatorsKEY HIGHLIGHTS
The Monthly Leading Economic Indicators report highlights changes in Consumer Price Indices,
inflation rates, interest rates and exchange rates. In addition, the report details changes in se-
lected indicators of international trade, agriculture, energy, manufacturing, construction, tourism
and transport.
MACROECONOMIC INDICATORS
The Consumer Price Index (CPI) decreased from an index of 140.23 in June 2024 to an index of
139.94 in July 2024, as shown in Table 1(a). The overall year on year inflation rate as measured
by the Consumer Price Index (CPI) was 4.3 per cent, in July 2024, as presented in Table 1(b).
The Kenyan Shilling depreciated against all major selected trading currencies, with the exception
of the Tanzanian Shilling, in July 2024, as outlined in Table 2. The average yield rate for 91-day
Treasury Bills, which is a benchmark for interest rate trends, rose from 15.97 per cent in June
2024 to 16.00 per cent in July 2024. The Central Bank of Kenya maintained its benchmark inter-
est rate at 13.0 per cent in July 2024. Interbank rate rose from 13.14 per cent in June 2024 to
13.17 per cent in July 2024. Commercial banks’ weighted average lending rate fell from 16.85
per cent in June 2024 to 16.84 per cent in July 2024, while the weighted average deposit rate
fell from 11.48 per cent to 11.28 per cent, during the same period, as shown in Table 3.
The NSE 20 Share index rose from 1,657 points in June 2024 to 1,670 points in July 2024. The
total number of shares traded grew from 279 million to 294 million, during the same period. The
total number of equity transactions increased from 23,076 in June 2024 to 26,039 in July 2024.
The Equities turnover increased from KSh 5.0 billion June 2024 to KSh 5.9 billion July 2024, as
outlined in Table 4.
The money supply (M1) grew from KSh 1.9 trillion in May 2024 to KSh 2.0 trillion in June 2024.
Money supply (M2) expanded from KSh 4.5 trillion to KSh 4.6 trillion during the same period.
Foreign currency deposits totalled KSh 1.3 trillion in June 2024. The broad money supply (M3)
expanded by 1.98 per cent, from KSh 5.9 trillion in May 2024 to KSh 6.0 trillion in June 2024, as
presented in Table 5(a). The value of Gross Foreign Exchange Reserves rose from KSh 2.0 trillion
in May 2024 to KSh 2.1 trillion in June 2024, while Net Foreign Exchange Liabilities rose from
KSh 0.8 trillion to KSh 0.9 trillion, during the same period, as shown in Table 5(b).
Pa ge | 1AGRICULTURE
The quantity of coffee auctioned at the Nairo-
bi Coffee Exchange rose from 3.5 thousand
metric tonnes (MT) in May 2024 to 4.5 thou-
sand MT in June 2024, accompanied by a
price increase from US Dollars (USD) 3.97 per
Kg to USD 4.33 per Kg, during the same peri-
od, as presented in Table 6.
Tea production fell from 54.5 thousand MT in May 2024 to 46.2 thousand MT in June 2024. The
average auction price for Kenya tea was lower at USD 2.14 per Kg in June 2024 compared to
USD 2.18 per Kg in May 2024. The average auction price for Kenya tea for the six months period
of the year 2024 stood at USD 2.20 per Kg, as shown in Table 7.
Cane deliveries rose from 887.6 thousand MT in June 2024 to 926.4 thousand MT in July 2024.
Notably, the cumulative total for the first seven months of 2024 reached 5,500.1 thousand MT,
surpassing the full-year deliveries of 5,483.6 thousand MT in 2023, as shown in Table 8. Tables 9
(b) and 9(d) present the average retail market prices for one Kg of maize and one Kg of beans,
respectively. The average monthly retail market price for one Kg of maize dropped from KSh
51.4 in June 2024 to KSh 51.1 in July 2024. Similarly, the average monthly retail market price for
one Kg of beans fell from KSh 150.8 to KSh 148.3, during the same period.
The quantity of coffee exported rose from 5.8 thousand MT in June 2024 to 7.0 thousand MT in
July 2024, with a corresponding increase in value from KSh 4.1 billion to KSh 4.9 billion. The
quantity of tea exported rose from 42.9 thousand MT in June 2024 to 54.3 thousand MT in July
2024, accompanied by an increase in value from KSh 11.8 billion to KSh 15.5 billion, as shown in
Table 10.
The volume of exported cut flowers dropped from 8.6 thousand MT in May 2024 to 6.7 thousand
MT in June 2024, with their value decreasing from KSh 6.6 billion to KSh 5.2 billion. The volume
of exported fruits rose from 21.7 thousand MT in May 2024 to 24.2 thousand MT in June 2024,
with a corresponding increase in value from KSh 4.6 billion to KSh 5.1 billion. The volume of ex-
ported vegetables was 4.2 thousand MT in June 2024, with a value of KSh 1.8 billion, as outlined
in Table 11.

KSh 323.1 billion in July 2024. Dur-

Uganda and the USA were Kenya's primary export destinations, with values of KSh 10.8 billion
and KSh 7.5 billion, respectively, in July 2024, as outlined in Table 13(a). Food and beverages
constituted the largest category of exports, accounting for 45.2 per cent. Non-food industrial
supplies made up 24.9 per cent, while machinery and other capital equipment constituted 1.9 per
cent of the total exports in July 2024, as presented in Table 13(b).
China and the United Arab Emirates were the leading importing partners, with values of KSh 59.7
billion and KSh 28.6 billion, respectively, in July 2024, as outlined in Table 14(a). The dominant
import category was non-food industrial supplies, constituting 36.1 per cent of the total imports.
Fuel and lubricants accounted for 22.2 per cent, machinery and other capital equipment consti-
tuted 15.5 per cent, while food and beverages accounted for 6.4 per cent of the total imports in
July 2024, as shown in Table 14(b).
The number of active mobile money agents rose from 334.0 thousand in June 2024 to 340.9
thousand in July 2024. Mobile money subscriptions increased from 77.9 million to 78.6 million.
Additionally, the total number of transactions grew from 201.1 million to 204.2 million during the
same period. The total value of transactions rose from KSh 676.0 billion in June 2024 to KSh
705.9 billion in July 2024, as detailed in Table 14(c). The export value of telecommunications
equipment surged fivefold, increasing from KSh 48.2 million in June 2024 to KSh 256.0 million in
July 2024. Concurrently, the import value more than doubled, rising from KSh 3.2 billion in June
2024 to KSh 6.6 billion in July 2024, as detailed in Table 14(d).
Pa ge | 3ENERGY, CONSTRUCTION AND MANUFACTURING
Local electricity generation increased from
1,020.2 million KWh in June 2024 to 1,098.9
million KWh in July 2024, as shown in Table 15
(a). Total electricity generation and imports
rose from 1,138.6 million KWh in June 2024 to
1,204.9 million KWh in July 2024. KPLC's total
sales grew from 875.3 million KWh in June
2024 to 901.8 million KWh in July 2024. Electricity imports declined from 118.4 million KWh to
106.0 million KWh, while electricity exports fell from 4.0 million KWh to 3.7 million KWh during
the same period, as outlined in Table 15(b).
The national average retail prices for motor gasoline premium, light diesel, and illuminating kero-
sene stood at KSh 189.46, KSh 172.33, and KSh 162.52 per litre, respectively, in July 2024. Char-
coal prices averaged KSh 84.41 per kg, while a 13-kg cylinder of gas averaged KSh 3,219.58,
during the same period, as shown in Table 15(e). On the international market, the OPEC crude
oil basket prices increased from US Dollars 83.22 per barrel in June 2024 to US Dollars 84.43 per
barrel in July 2024, as presented in Table 15(f).
The value of approved residential buildings by Nairobi City County fell from KSh 25.3 billion in
June 2024 to KSh 12.2 billion in July 2024. Conversely, the value of approved non-residential
buildings rose from KSh 4.0 billion to KSh 5.2 billion during the same period, as shown in Table
16.
Sugar production grew from 75.5 thousand MT in June 2024 to 83.8 thousand MT in July 2024,
as presented in Table 17(a). Production of soft drinks rose from 40.2 million litres in June 2024
to 40.5 million litres in July 2024, as outlined in Table 17(b). Production of assembled vehicles
rose from 1,077 units in June 2024 to 1,194 units in July 2024, as presented in Table 17(c). Pro-
duction of galvanized sheets fell from 22.5 thousand MT in June 2024 to 21.9 thousand MT in
July 2024, as shown in Table 17(d). The quantity of cement produced rose from 706.9 thousand
MT in March 2024 to 736.1 thousand MT in April 2024. In addition, cement consumption rose
from 653.7 thousand MT to 701.5 thousand MT during the same period, as detailed in Table 17
(e). Milk intake in the formal sector dropped from 77.8 million litres in June 2024 to 74.4 million
litres in July 2024, as shown in Table 17(f).
Pa ge | 4TOURISM AND TRANSPORT
The total number of visitor arrivals at Jo-
mo Kenyatta International Airport (JKIA)
and Moi International Airport (MIA) rose
from 117,639 persons in May 2024 to
149,922 persons in June 2024. Notably,
arrivals at JKIA increased from 111,925
persons to 140,617 persons during the same period, as presented in Table 18.
Tables 19(a) and 19(b) present embarked passengers by port of destination and landed passen-
gers by port of origin through JKIA, respectively. The number of embarked passengers by port of
destination rose from 259,304 in May 2024 to 283,104 in June 2024. Similarly, the number of
passengers who landed by port of origin rose from 257,876 to 296,807 during the same period.
The total number of newly registered motor vehicles and motorcycles, categorized by body type,
dropped from 18,509 units in February 2024 to 15,984 units in March 2024. The number of newly
registered motorcycles dropped from 8,561 units to 6,509 units during the same period, as
shown in Table 19(c).
The number of passengers traveling by the Standard Gauge Railway (SGR) rose from 168,525 in
May 2024 to 186,920 in June 2024, with a corresponding increase in revenue from KSh 279.3
million to KSh 307.0 million. Cargo volume transported via SGR dropped from 615.6 thousand
tonnes to 562.4 thousand tonnes, with revenue declining from KSh 1.3 billion to KSh 1.0 billion
during the same period, as presented in Table 19(d). Metre Gauge Railway (MGR) passenger us-
age rose from 140,840 in May 2024 to 182,332 in June 2024. Additionally, revenue from MGR
passenger traffic increased from KSh 7.4 million to KSh 9.5 million during the same period, as
presented in Table 19(e).
Table 19(f) presents Mombasa Port throughput. The volume of cargo handled decreased from 3.4
million MT in June 2024 to 3.3 million MT in July 2024. The export volume surged by 21.4 per
cent, increasing from 415.1 thousand MT in June 2024 to 504.2 thousand MT in July 2024. The
import volume declined from 2.5 million MT in June 2024 to 2.3 million MT in July 2024. Trans-
shipment grew by 1.3 per cent from 454.3 thousand MT in June 2024 to 460.2 thousand MT in
July 2024. Additionally, restows edged up slightly from 10.3 thousand MT to 10.4 thousand MT
during the same period.
