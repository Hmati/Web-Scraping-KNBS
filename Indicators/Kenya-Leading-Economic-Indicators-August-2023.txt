iiKEY HIGHLIGHTS
The Monthly Leading Economic Indicators report highlights changes in Consumer Price Indices,
inflation, interest rates and exchange rates. In addition, the report details changes in selected
indicators of international trade, agriculture, energy, manufacturing, building and construction,
tourism and transport.
MACROECONOMIC INDICATORS
The Consumer Price Index (CPI) decreased by 0.1 per cent from an index of 134.15 in July
2023 to 134.02 in August 2023 as presented in Table 1(a). The overall inflation as measured by
the Consumer Price Indices decreased from 7.3 per cent to 6.7 per cent over the same period
as shown in Table 1(b).
Table 2 presents mean monthly foreign exchange rates of Kenyan Shilling against selected ma-
jor trading currencies. In August 2023, the Kenyan Shilling depreciated against selected major
trading currencies except against the Japanese Yen , South African Rand and Tanzanian Shil-
ling.
The average yield rate for the 91-day Treasury Bills, which is a benchmark for the general trend
of interest rates rose from 12.12 per cent in July 2023 to 13.25 per cent in August 2023. The
Central Bank Rate (CBR) was 10.50 in August 2023.The Interbank rates rose from 10.34 per
cent in July 2023 to 12.57 per cent in August 2023 as shown in Table 3.
The Nairobi Securities Exchange (NSE) 20 share index decreased from 1,577 points in July
2023 to 1,540 points in August 2023. During the same period, the total number of shares traded
decreased from 417 million to 241 million. The number of equity transactions increased from
23,569 in July to 23,885 in August 2023. Equities Market turnover decreased from KSh 7.62 bil-
lion in July 2023 to KSh 4.88 billion in August 2023, as presented in Table 4.
Table 5(a) presents Money Supply statistics. Broad money supply (M3), a key indicator for mon-
etary policy formulation, decreased from KSh 5,568.05 billion in June 2023 to KSh 5,148.50 bil-
lion in July 2023. The value of Gross Foreign Exchange Reserves increased from KSh 1,859.64
billion in June 2023 to KSh 1,946.75 billion in July 2023. During the same period, the value of
Net Foreign Exchange Reserves increased from KSh 594.10 billion to KSh 628.93 billion as pre-
sented in Table 5(b).
3AGRICULTURE
The quantity of coffee sold decreased from 4,407.45 MT in April 2023 to 1,373.50 MT in May
2023 as presented in Table 6. The quantity of produced tea decreased from 57,885.91 MT in
May 2023 to 48,127.89 MT in June 2023.The average auction price for Kenya tea in the month
of June 2023 was lower at KSh 291.87 per Kg against KSh 300.45 per Kg in May 2023, as pre-
sented in Table 7.
The quantity of cane deliveries decreased from 395.23 thousand metric tonnes in July 2023 to
295.81 thousand metric tonnes in August 2023,as presented in Table 8.The average monthly
retail market prices for a one Kg of maize decreased from KSh 85.7 in July 2023 to KSh 74.8 in
August 2023 as presented in Table 9(b). The average monthly retail market prices for a one Kg
of beans decreased from KSh 173.3 in July 2023 to KSh 168.1 in August 2023 as shown in Ta-
ble 9(d).
Quantity of coffee exported decreased from 6,174.90 MT in July 2023 to 4,652.03 MT in August
2023. Additionally, its value decreased from KSh 4.19 billion to KSh 3.26 billion over the same
period as shown in Table 10. The quantity of tea exported increased from 39,943.62 MT in July
2023 to 58,590.66 MT in August 2023. In addition, its value increased from KSh 12.91 billion to
KSh 20.19 billion over the same period as presented in Table 10.
Statistics on monthly export of fresh horticultural produce are presented in Table 11. The quan-
tity of Cut Flowers exported increased from 8,931.54 MT in June 2023 to 11,715.32 MT in July
2023. Additionally, the value of Cut Flowers exported increased from KSh 5,186.30 million to
KSh 6,926.53 million during the same period. The quantity of Fruits exported increased from
19,518.58 MT in June 2023 to 22,916.07 MT in July 2023. The value of fruits exported in-
creased from KSh 3,009.73 million to KSh 3,702.47 million during the same period. The quanti-
ty of Vegetables exported was 5,254.93 MT, while their value was KSh 1,565.52 million in July
2023.

In August 2023, Kenya’s export major trading partners were Pakistan with a value of KSh 9.86
billion followed by Uganda with a value of KSh 9.67 billion, as presented in Table 13
(a).Domestic exports by Broad Economic Category indicated that food and beverages was the
main export category in August 2023 accounting for 49.32 per cent of the domestic exports.
Industrial supplies accounted for 24.32 per cent of the domestic exports. Major origins of im-
ports as presented in Table 14(a) indicates that United Arabs Emirates was the main import
partner with a value of KSh 50.05 billion followed by China with a value of KSh 38.69 billion in
August 2023.Imports by Broad Economic Category indicate that non-food industrial supplies
were the main import category in August 2023 with a share of 30.92 per cent of the total im-
ports in August 2023. Food and Beverages accounted for 16.89 per cent of the total imports
during the same period, as presented in Table 14(b).
The total number of mobile money transactions increased from 197.42 million in June 2023 to
202.85 million in July 2023. Similarly, the value of the transactions increased from KSh 643.76
billion to 684.64 billion during the same period. The number of Mobile Money Agents increased
from 328,543 in June 2023 to 330,912 in July 2023. During the same period, total registered
mobile money subscriptions increased from 76.99 million to 77.21 million as presented in Table
14(c). As shown in Table 14(d), the value of imports for telecommunication equipment regis-
tered a threefold increase from KSh 1.50 billion in June 2023 to KSh 4.49 billion in July 2023.
5ENERGY AND MANUFACTURING
Monthly electricity statistics are presented in Tables 15(a) and 15(b). Total local electricity gen-
eration decreased from 1,158.85 million KWh in July 2023 to 1,154.22 million KWh in August
2023.Geo thermal was leading in generation with 522.92 million KWh followed by Hydro with
258.72 million KWh in August 2023. Total electricity exported increased from 2.91 million KWh
in July 2023 to 4.45 million KWh in August 2023.
The national average domestic retail oil prices of motor gasoline premium, light diesel and ker-
osene was KSh 195.32, KSh 180.42 and KSh 170.25 per litre respectively in August 2023 as
shown in Table 15(e). Charcoal prices averaged KSh 70.73 per Kg while the price of a 13-Kg
cylinder of gas averaged KSh 2,709.07 during the same period. In the international market, the
price of the OPEC crude oil basket increased from US Dollars 81.06 per barrel in July 2023 to
US Dollars 87.33 per barrel in August 2023 as shown in Table 15(f).
Tables 17(a) to 17(f) present various monthly manufacturing statistics. Sugar production de-
creased cent from 33,246 MT in July 2023 to 27,680 MT in August 2023.Production of soft
drinks increased from 38,269 thousand litres in May 2023 to 42,192 thousand litres in June
2023.Production of assembled vehicles increased from 906 units in June 2023 to 974 units in
July 2023.Production of Galvanized sheets increased from 21,100 metric tonnes in June 2023
to 23,930 metric tonnes in July 2023.
The quantity of cement produced decreased from 803,317 MT in June 2023 to 783,117 MT in
July 2023. Additionally, consumption of cement decreased from 772,376 MT to 742,443 MT
during the same period. Milk intake in the formal sector decreased from 73.78 million litres in
May 2023 to 71.69 million litres in June 2023.
6TOURISM AND TRANSPORT
The total number of visitors arriving through Jomo Kenyatta (JKIA) and Moi International Air-
ports (MIA) increased from 132,297 persons in June 2023 to 158,100 persons in July 2023, as
shown in Table 18.
Table 19(a) shows international passenger air traffic departures through Jomo Kenyatta Inter-
national Airport. The number of international departures increased by 16.4 per cent from
250,343 in June 2023 to 291,369 in July 2023.Table 19(b) presents international passenger air
traffic arrivals through Jomo Kenyatta International Airport. The number of passengers in-
creased by 13.5 per cent from 263,534 in June 2023 to 299,108 in July 2023.
The number of passengers transported via Standard Gauge Railway (SGR) decreased by 7.2
per cent from 214,050 in May 2023 to 198,564 in June 2023. The drop in passengers resulted
in a 6.9 per cent decline in revenue generated to KSh 218.7 million in June. The volume of car-
go transported by SGR declined slightly by 0.2 per cent while the revenue generated during the
period under review decreased by 11.1 per cent to KSh 1,139.0 million in June as shown in Ta-
ble 19(d).
Table 19 (e) represents Mombasa Port throughput .The volume of cargo handled registered a
5.7 per cent increase from 2,796.5 thousand tonnes in July 2023 to 2,955.4 thousand tonnes in
August 2023. Imports during the same month period rose by 7.9 per cent to 2,363.3 thousand
tonnes in August 2023 with the highest rise occasioned by a doubling in volume of Petroleum
Oil Product imports. Domestic exports experienced a 9.3 per cent dip from 464.3 thousand
tonnes in July 2023 to 421.3 thousand tonnes in August 2023. The proportion of export to total
volume handled decline from 16.6 per cent in July 2023 to 14.3 per cent in August 2023.
The number of passengers transported through Metre Gauge Railway (MGR) declined by 5.4
per cent from 308,044 in May to 291,322 in June 2023. Revenue generated via MGR dropped
marginally from KSh 15.1 million in May to KSh 14.9 million in June 2023 as shown in Table 19
(f).
