iiKEY HIGHLIGHTS
The Monthly Leading Economic Indicators report highlights changes in Consumer Price Indices,
inflation rates, interest rates and exchange rates. In addition, the report details changes in se-
lected indicators of international trade, agriculture, energy, manufacturing, construction, tourism
and transport.
MACROECONOMIC INDICATORS
The Consumer Price Index (CPI) decreased from an index of 138.66 in March 2024 to an index
of 138.40 in April 2024, as indicated in Table 1(a). The overall year on year inflation rate as
measured by the Consumer Price Index (CPI) was 5.0 per cent, in April 2024, as presented in
Table 1(b).
The Kenyan Shilling appreciated against all major selected trading currencies in April 2024, as
outlined in Table 2. The average yield rate for 91-day Treasury Bills, which is a benchmark for
interest rate trends, rose from 16.51 per cent in February 2024 to 16.68 per cent in March 2024.
The Central Bank of Kenya maintained its benchmark interest rate at 13.0 in April 2024. Inter-
bank rates dropped from 13.59 per cent in February 2024 to 13.42 per cent in March 2024, as
presented in Table 3.
The NSE 20 Share index declined by 3.5 per cent from 1,752 points in March 2024 to 1,691
points in April 2024. The total volume of shares traded decreased from 670 million to 301 million
during the same period. The total number of equity transactions increased from 25,008 in March
2024 to 27,190 in April 2024. The Equities Market turnover decreased by 37.7 per cent to KSh
7.3 billion in April 2024, compared to the KSh 11.8 billion recorded in March 2024, as outlined in
Table 4.
Tables 5(a) and 5(b) present statistics on Money Supply. Broad money supply (M3) declined by
1.71 per cent, from KSh 6,111.1 billion in January 2024 to KSh 6,006.4 billion in February 2024.
Similarly, foreign currency deposits decreased from KSh 1,603.2 billion to KSh 1,504.1 billion
during the same period. The value of Gross Foreign Exchange Reserves decreased from KSh
2,431.0 billion in January 2024 to KSh 2,391.6 billion in February 2024. Net Foreign Exchange
Liabilities increased from KSh 857.8 billion to KSh 886.5 billion during the same period.

The quantity of coffee auctioned
at the Nairobi Coffee Exchange
rose from 4,824.0 MT in Febru-
ary 2024 to 5,070.0 MT in
March 2024. The price of coffee
auctioned dropped from US Dol-
lars 5.09 per Kg to US Dollars
4.84 per Kg, during the same
period, as presented in Table 6. Tea production decreased from 58,966.9 MT in January 2024, to
55,447.3 MT in February 2024. The average auction price for Kenya tea was higher at 2.23 US
Dollars per Kg in February 2024 compared to 2.19 US Dollars per Kg in January 2024, as shown
in Table 7.
Cane deliveries rose by 10.4 per cent from 756.0 thousand MT in February 2024 to 834.9 thou-
sand MT in March 2024, as shown in Table 8. Tables 9(b) and 9(d) present the average retail
market prices for one Kg of maize and one Kg of beans, respectively. The average monthly retail
market price for one Kg of maize decreased from KSh 57.5 in March 2024 to KSh 54.4 in April
2024. Similarly, the average monthly retail market price for one Kg of beans dropped from KSh
156.9 to KSh 153.8, during the same period.
The quantity of exported coffee increased from 4,291.3 MT in March 2024 to 5,370.5 MT in April
2024, with a corresponding increase in value from KSh 3.3 billion to KSh 3.9 billion, during the
same period. The quantity of exported tea decreased from 59,599.2 MT in March 2024 to
56,263.4 MT in April 2024. In addition, its value dropped from KSh 19.1 billion to KSh 17.3 billion
during the same period, as outlined in Table 10.
Table 11 provides data on the monthly exports of fresh horticultural produce. The volume of ex-
ported cut flowers decreased from 12,841.9 MT in November 2023 to 4,684.6 MT in December
2023, accompanied by a corresponding decrease in value from KSh 9.5 billion to KSh 3.2 billion.
Similarly, the volume of exported fruits declined from 3,474.9 MT in November 2023 to 1,699.5
MT in December 2023, with a decrease in value from KSh 0.6 billion to KSh 0.2 billion during the
same period. The volume of exported vegetables decreased from 14,599.8 MT in November 2023
to 4,777.6 MT in December 2023, with a corresponding decline in value from KSh 7.5 billion to
KSh 1.4 billion.
4INTERNATIONAL TRADE AND ICT
Table 12 presents monthly statistics on ex-
ternal trade. The volume of trade rose
from KSh 306.2 billion in March 2024 to
KSh 329.1 billion in April 2024. The total
export value decreased from KSh 97.9 bil-
lion to KSh 92.9 billion during the same
period. The total import value increased
from KSh 208.2 billion in March 2024 to KSh 236.2 billion in April 2024.
In April 2024, Uganda and Pakistan were Kenya's primary export destinations, with values of KSh
8.9 billion and KSh 7.6 billion, respectively, as outlined in Table 13(a). Food and beverages con-
stituted the largest category of exports, accounting for 47.9 per cent, while non-food industrial
supplies made up 24.5 per cent of the total exports, as presented in Table 13(b).
Table 14(a) outlines the main origins of imports. China and the United Arab Emirates were the
leading importing partners with values of KSh 42.5 billion and KSh 24.5 billion respectively, in
April 2024. The dominant import category was non-food industrial supplies, constituting 33.6 per
cent of the total imports. Fuel and lubricants accounted for 22.5 per cent of the total imports,
while food and beverages accounted for 11.8 per cent of the total imports during the same peri-
od, as shown in Table 14(b).
The number of mobile money agents decreased from 321,340 in January 2024 to 320,182 in
February 2024. The number of mobile money subscriptions increased from 76.8 million to 77.3
million. In addition, the total number of transactions grew from 211.6 million to 213.3 million,
during the same period. The total value of transactions rose from KSh 784.0 billion in January
2024 to KSh 790.8 billion in February 2024, as shown in Table 14(c). The import value of tele-
communication equipment more than doubled from KSh 2.4 billion in March 2024 to KSh 4.9 bil-
lion in April 2024, as detailed in Table 14(d).
5ENERGY, CONSTRUCTION AND MANUFACTURING
Tables 15(a) and 15(b) present monthly
electricity statistics. Local electricity gen-
eration dropped from 1,052.0 million
KWh in March 2024 to 997.8 million KWh
in April 2024. Geo-thermal was the lead-
ing source of electricity generation in
April 2024, accounting for 42.3 per cent. Notably, Hydro generation rose by 24.5 per cent to
362.4 million KWh in April 2024, from 291.1 million KWh in March 2024. Total electricity genera-
tion and imports decreased from 1,180.4 million KWh in March 2024 to 1,126.7 million KWh in
April 2024. Total sales by KPLC went up from 878.9 million KWh to 883.0 million KWh. Total elec-
tricity imports increased from 128.4 million KWh to 128.9 million KWh. Total electricity exports
decreased from 3.9 million KWh to 3.5 million KWh, during the same period.
The national average retail prices for motor gasoline premium, light diesel, and illuminating kero-
sene stood at KSh 194.46, KSh 181.11, and KSh 171.18 per litre, respectively, in April 2024.
Charcoal prices averaged KSh 79.50 per kg, while a 13-kg cylinder of gas averaged KSh 3,242.11,
during the same period, as shown in Table 15(e). On the international market, the OPEC crude
oil basket price increased from US Dollars 84.22 per barrel in March 2024 to US Dollars 89.12 per
barrel in April 2024, as presented in Table 15(f).
The total value of buildings approved by Nairobi City County increased from KSh 13.6 billion in
March 2024 to KSh 16.4 billion in April 2024. The value of approved residential buildings rose
from KSh 11.6 billion to KSh 13.8 billion. Similarly, the value of approved non-residential build-
ings rose from KSh 2.0 billion to KSh 2.6 billion during the same period, as outlined in Table 16.
Sugar production increased by 10.2 per cent in March 2024, reaching 69,520 MT, up from 63,075
MT in February 2024, as indicated in Table 17(a). Cement production decreased from 711,663
MT in February 2024 to 706,871 MT in March 2024, while cement consumption rose from
653,445 MT to 653,700 MT during the same period, as detailed in Table 17(e). Milk intakes in the
formal sector declined from 70.3 million litres in March 2024 to 66.9 million litres in April 2024, as
shown in Table 17(f).
6TOURISM AND TRANSPORT
The total number of arrivals
through both Jomo Kenyatta
(JKIA) and Moi International Air-
ports (MIA) dropped from 146,442
persons in February 2024 to
128,057 persons in March 2024, as
shown in Table 18.
Tables 19(a) and 19(b) present embarked passengers by port of destination and by port of origin,
through Jomo Kenyatta International Airport, respectively. The number of embarked passengers
by port of destination rose from 256,525 in February 2024 to 266,893 in March 2024. Conversely,
the number of landed passengers by port of origin dropped from 253,717 to 251,616 during the
same period. The total number of newly registered motor vehicles, categorized by body type, de-
clined from 18,509 units in February 2024 to 15,984 units in March 2024. Notably, the number of
newly registered motorcycles dropped significantly from 8,561 units to 6,509 units during the
same period, as shown in Table 19(c).
The number of passengers traveling by the Standard Gauge Railway (SGR) rose from 158,652 in
February 2024 to 180,645 in March 2024, with a corresponding revenue increase from KSh 270.4
million to KSh 298.0 million. Cargo volume transported via SGR rose from 342,867 tonnes to
449,200 tonnes, with a drop in revenue from KSh 981.1 million to KSh 976.1 million during the
same period, as presented in Table 19(d). Metre Gauge Railway (MGR) passenger usage rose
from 240,623 in February 2024 to 242,976 in March 2024. In addition, revenue generated from
MGR passenger traffic rose from KSh 14.8 million to KSh 15.5 million during the same period, as
presented in Table 19(e).
Table 19(f) presents Mombasa Port throughput. The volume of cargo handled grew from 2,741.9
thousand MT in February 2024 to 3,336.2 thousand MT in March 2024. Import volume rose from
1,877.6 thousand MT in February 2024 to 2,375.8 thousand MT in March 2024. Additionally, ex-
port volume rose from 371.4 thousand MT to 494.2 thousand MT. Transshipment rose by 0.1 per
cent to 457.7 thousand MT, while restows recorded a significant drop of 76.1 per cent to 8.5
thousand MT from 35.6 thousand MT, during the same period.
