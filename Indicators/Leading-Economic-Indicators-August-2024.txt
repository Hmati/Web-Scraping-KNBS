Leading Economic IndicatorsKEY HIGHLIGHTS
The Monthly Leading Economic Indicators report highlights changes in Consumer Price Indices,
inflation rates, interest rates and exchange rates. In addition, the report details changes in se-
lected indicators of international trade, agriculture, energy, manufacturing, construction, tourism
and transport.
MACROECONOMIC INDICATORS
The Consumer Price Index (CPI) decreased from an index of 139.94 in July 2024 to an index of
139.87 in August 2024, as shown in Table 1(a). The overall year on year inflation rate as meas-
ured by the CPI was 4.4 per cent, in August 2024, as presented in Table 1(b).
The Kenyan Shilling appreciated against the US Dollar, Tanzanian Shilling, and Ugandan Shilling,
but depreciated against the Sterling Pound, Euro, Japanese Yen, and South African Rand, in Au-
gust 2024, as shown in Table 2. The average yield rate for 91-day Treasury Bills, which is a
benchmark for interest rate trends, fell from 16.00 per cent in July 2024 to 15.85 per cent in Au-
gust 2024. The Central Bank of Kenya lowered its benchmark interest rate from 13.00 per cent in
July 2024 to 12.75 per cent in August 2024. Interbank rate fell from 13.17 per cent in July 2024
to 13.01 per cent in August 2024. Commercial banks’ weighted average lending rate stood at
16.84 per cent in August 2024, while the weighted average deposit rate rose from 11.28 per
cent in July 2024 to 11.59 per cent in August 2024. Additionally, overdraft rates decreased from
16.43 per cent to 16.39 per cent, while savings rates rose from 4.56 per cent to 4.62 per cent
during the same period, as shown in Table 3.
The NSE 20 Share Index rose from 1,670 in July 2024 to 1,678 points in August 2024. Concur-
rently, the total number of shares traded surged from 294 million to 392 million. The number of
equity transactions fell from 26.0 thousand to 25.6 thousand, while equity turnover grew from
KSh 5.9 billion to KSh 6.5 billion during the same period, as detailed in Table 4.
The money supply (M1) grew from KSh 1.9 trillion in May 2024 to KSh 2.0 trillion in June 2024.
Money supply (M2) rose from KSh 4.5 trillion to KSh 4.6 trillion Money supply (M3) expanded by
1.98 per cent, from KSh 5.9 trillionto KSh 6.0 trillion during the same period, as presented in Ta-
ble 5(a). The value of Gross Foreign Exchange Reserves rose from KSh 2.0 trillion in May 2024 to
KSh 2.1 trillion in June 2024, while Net Foreign Exchange Liabilities rose from KSh 0.8 trillion to
KSh 0.9 trillion during the same period, as shown in Table 5(b).

The quantity of coffee auctioned at
the Nairobi Coffee Exchange rose
from 3.5 thousand metric tonnes (MT)
in May 2024 to 4.5 thousand MT in
June 2024, accompanied by a price
increase from US Dollars (USD) 3.97
per kilogram (Kg) to USD 4.33 per Kg
during the same period, as presented in Table 6.
Tea production fell from 54.5 thousand MT in May 2024 to 46.2 thousand MT in June 2024. The
volume of tea auctioned rose from 28.5 thousand MT in June 2024 to 34.1 thousand MT in July
2024. During the same period, the average auction price for Kenyan tea rose from USD 2.14 per
kg to USD 2.21 per kg. Over the first seven months of 2024, the average auction price for Ken-
yan tea stood at USD 2.20 per kg, as shown in Table 7.
Cane deliveries fell from 926.4 thousand MT in July 2024 to 800.3 thousand MT in August 2024,
as shown in Table 8. Tables 9(b) and 9(d) present the average retail market prices for one Kg of
maize and one Kg of beans, respectively. The average monthly retail market price for one Kg of
maize dropped from KSh 50.4 in July 2024 to KSh 48.7 in August 2024. Similarly, the average
monthly retail market price for one Kg of beans fell from KSh 149.8 to KSh 147.4 during the
same period.
The quantity of coffee exported fell from 7.0 thousand MT in July 2024 to 5.7 thousand MT in
August 2024, with the export value fell from KSh 4.9 billion to KSh 3.7 billion. Similarly, the
quantity of tea exported fell from 54.3 thousand MT in July to 50.8 thousand MT in August 2024,
accompanied by a drop in value from KSh 15.5 billion to KSh 14.7 billion, as shown in Table 10.
The volume of exported cut flowers decreased from 8.6 thousand MT in May 2024 to 6.7 thou-
sand MT in June 2024, with their value falling from KSh 6.6 billion to KSh 5.2 billion. The volume
of exported fruits increased in volume from 21.7 thousand MT in May 2024 to 24.2 thousand MT
in June 2024, accompanied by an increase in value from KSh 4.6 billion to KSh 5.1 billion. In ad-
dition, the volume of exported vegetables slightly rose to 4.2 thousand MT in June 2024, with an
increase in value from KSh 1.6 billion to KSh 1.8 billion, as outlined in Table 11.
Pa ge | 2INTERNATIONAL TRADE AND ICT
The total volume of trade declined from KSh
323.1 billion in July 2024 to KSh 319.9 billion
in August 2024. During the same period, ex-
port value fell from KSh 97.0 billion to KSh
93.4 billion, while import value increased from
KSh 226.1 billion to KSh 226.5 billion, as
shown in Table 12.
Uganda and Pakistan were Kenya's primary export destinations in August 2024, with export val-
ues of KSh 9.9 billion and KSh 7.3 billion, respectively, as outlined in Table 13(a). The dominant
export category was food and beverages, constituting 43.3 per cent of the total exports, followed
by non-food industrial supplies at 26.7 per cent. Machinery and other capital equipment account-
ed for 2.1 per cent of the total exports, as presented in Table 13(b).
China and the United Arab Emirates were the leading importing partners in August 2024, with
import values of KSh 57.2 billion and KSh 31.0 billion, respectively, as outlined in Table 14(a).
The predominant import category was non-food industrial supplies, accounting for 30.8 per cent
of the total imports. Fuel and lubricants made up 22.2 per cent, transport equipment represented
13.5 per cent, machinery and other capital equipment constituted 13.1 per cent, while food and
beverages accounted for 10.1 per cent of the total imports, as shown in Table 14(b).
The number of active mobile money agents grew from 340,889 in July 2024 to 348,065 in August
2024. Mobile money subscriptions increased from 78.61 million to 78.64 million. Transaction vol-
ume expanded significantly, with the total number of transactions rising from 204.17 million to
214.25 million. Concurrently, the total value of transactions grew from KSh 705.85 billion to KSh
705.91 billion during the same period, as outlined in Table 14(c). The export value of telecom-
munications equipment dropped from KSh 256.0 million in July 2024 to KSh 119.3 million in Au-
gust 2024, Similarly, their import value decreased significantly from KSh 6.6 billion to KSh 2.7
billion. Notably, the import value of automatic data processing machines and storage units fell by
63.6 per cent from KSh 2.6 billion to KSh 0.9 billion. In contrast, their export value recorded a
modest growth of 0.8 per cent, increasing from KSh 32.1 million to KSh 32.4 million during the
same period, as detailed in Table 14(d).
Pa ge | 3ENERGY, CONSTRUCTION AND MANUFACTURING
Local electricity generation dropped from
1,098.9 million kilowatt-hours (KWh) in
July 2024 to 1,096.2 million KWh in Au-
gust 2024, as shown in Table 15(a). To-
tal electricity generation and imports
rose from 1,204.9 million KWh in July
2024 to 1,227.3 million KWh in August
2024, driven by a rise in electricity im-
ports from 106.0 million KWh to 131.1 million KWh. During the same period, KPLC's total sales
grew from 901.8 million KWh to 923.1 million KWh, while electricity exports fell from 3.7 million
KWh to 2.7 million KWh, as outlined in Table 15(b).
The national average retail prices for motor gasoline premium, light diesel oil, and illuminating
kerosene averaged KSh 189.46, KSh 172.33, and KSh 162.52 per litre, respectively, in August
2024. Charcoal prices averaged KSh 84.17 per kg, while a 13-kg cylinder of gas averaged KSh
3,217.85, during the same period, as shown in Table 15(e). On the international market, the
OPEC crude oil basket prices decreased from USD 84.43 per barrel in July 2024 to USD 78.41 per
barrel in August 2024, as presented in Table 15(f).
The value of approved residential buildings by Nairobi City County rose from KSh 12.2 billion in
July 2024 to KSh 15.7 billion in August 2024. The value of approved non-residential buildings
dropped from KSh 5.2 billion to KSh 2.5 billion during the same period, as detailed in Table 16.
Sugar production fell from 83.8 thousand MT in July 2024 to 73.4 thousand MT in August 2024.
However, the cumulative sugar output for the first eight months of 2024 totalled 541.7 thousand
MT, exceeding the entire production of 472.8 thousand MT recorded in 2023, as detailed in Table
17(a). Production of soft drinks rose from 40.7 million litres in July 2024 to 64.5 million litres in
August 2024, as outlined in Table 17(b). Production of assembled vehicles decreased from 1,194
units in July 2024 to 1,007 units in August 2024, as presented in Table 17(c). Production of gal-
vanized sheets rose from 21.9 thousand MT in July 2024 to 24.9 thousand MT in August 2024, as
presented in Table 17(d). Milk intake in the formal sector dropped from 74.4 million litres in July
2024 to 74.2 million litres in August 2024, as shown in Table 17(f).
Pa ge | 4TOURISM AND TRANSPORT
The total number of visitors arrivals at Jomo
Kenyatta International Airport (JKIA) and Moi
International Airport (MIA) rose from 169,722
persons in July 2024 to 175,113 persons in
August 2024. Notably, MIA saw a substantial
increase of 25.8 per cent, growing from
15,761 persons to 19,822 persons during the same period, as shown in Table 18.
Tables 19(a) and 19(b) present embarked passengers by port of destination and landed passen-
gers by port of origin through JKIA, respectively. The number of embarked passengers by port of
destination rose from 259,304 in May 2024 to 283,104 in June 2024. Similarly, the number of
passengers who landed by port of origin rose from 257,876 to 296,807 during the same period.
The total number of newly registered motor vehicles and motorcycles, categorized by body type,
decreased from 13,499 units in July 2024 to 9,888 units in August 2024. During the same period,
the number of newly registered motorcycles fell from 6,060 units to 4,292 units, as indicated in
Table 19(c).
The number of passengers traveling by the Standard Gauge Railway (SGR) rose from 168,525 in
May 2024 to 186,920 in June 2024, with a corresponding increase in revenue from KSh 279.3
million to KSh 307.0 million. Cargo volume transported via SGR dropped from 615.6 thousand
tonnes to 562.4 thousand tonnes, with revenue declining from KSh 1.3 billion to KSh 1.0 billion
during the same period, as presented in Table 19(d). Metre Gauge Railway (MGR) passenger us-
age rose from 140,840 in May 2024 to 182,332 in June 2024. Additionally, revenue from MGR
passenger traffic increased from KSh 7.4 million to KSh 9.5 million during the same period, as
presented in Table 19(e).
Table 19(f) presents Mombasa Port throughput. The volume of cargo handled decreased from 3.4
million MT in June 2024 to 3.3 million MT in July 2024. The export volume surged by 21.4 per
cent, increasing from 415.1 thousand MT in June 2024 to 504.2 thousand MT in July 2024. The
import volume declined from 2.5 million MT in June 2024 to 2.3 million MT in July 2024. Trans-
shipment grew by 1.3 per cent from 454.3 thousand MT in June 2024 to 460.2 thousand MT in
July 2024. Additionally, restows edged up slightly from 10.3 thousand MT to 10.4 thousand MT
during the same period.
