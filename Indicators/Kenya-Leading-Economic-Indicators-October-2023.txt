iiKEY HIGHLIGHTS
The Monthly Leading Economic Indicators report highlights changes in Consumer Price Indices,
inflation, interest rates and exchange rates. In addition, the report details changes in selected
indicators of international trade, agriculture, energy, manufacturing, building and construction,
tourism and transport.
MACROECONOMIC INDICATORS
The Consumer Price Index (CPI) increased by 1.0 per cent, from an index of 135.32 in Septem-
ber 2023 to an index of 136.71 in October 2023, as indicated in Table 1(a). The overall inflation,
as measured by the Consumer Price Indices, increased from 6.8 per cent to 6.9 per cent during
the same period, as presented in Table 1(b).
Table 2 provides the mean monthly foreign exchange rates of the Kenyan Shilling against major
selected trading currencies. In October 2023, the Kenyan Shilling depreciated against selected
major trading currencies except against the Sterling Pound.
The average yield rate for 91-day Treasury Bills, a significant indicator of interest rate trends,
rose from 14.38 per cent in September 2023 to 14.96 per cent in October 2023. The Central
Bank of Kenya maintained its benchmark interest rate at 10.50 per cent in October 2023. The
Inter-bank rates went up from 12.36 per cent in September 2023 to 12.70 per cent in October
2023, as presented in Table 3.
The Nairobi Securities Exchange (NSE) 20 share index decreased from 1,508 points in Sep-
tember 2023 to 1,461 points in October 2023. The total volume of shares traded declined from
424 million to 329 million during the same period. The number of equity transactions decreased
from 20,474 in September 2023 to 20,127 in October 2023. The Equities Market turnover de-
creased by 7.3 per cent to KSh 4.8 billion in October 2023 compared to KSh 5.2 billion recorded
in the month of September 2023, as outlined in Table 4.
Tables 5(a) provides statistics on Money Supply. Broad money supply (M3), a key indicator for
monetary policy formulation, increased from KSh 5,774.6 billion in August 2023 to KSh 5,841.1
billion in September 2023. Foreign currency deposits rose from KSh 1,328.6 billion in August
2023 to KSh 1,399.1 billion in September 2023. Gross foreign exchange reserves increased
from KSh 1,115.5 billion in August 2023 to KSh 1,121.5 billion in September 2023. Similarly, the
value of Net foreign exchange liabilities increased from KSh 675.2 billion to KSh 711.2 billion
during the same period, as presented in Table 5(b).
3AGRICULTURE
The quantity of coffee sold increased from 154.2 MT in August 2023 to 339.9 MT in September
2023, as shown in Table 6. The production of tea rose from 45,577.7 MT in August 2023 to
48,497.3 MT in September 2023. During the nine-month period of 2023, the average auction
price for Kenya tea was USD 2.25 per Kg as shown in Table 7.
Cane deliveries rose from 183.0 thousand metric tonnes in September 2023 to 257.4 thousand
metric tonnes in October 2023, as presented in Table 8. The average monthly retail market
price for one Kg of maize decreased from KSh 70.1 in September 2023 to KSh 70.0 in October
2023, as shown in Table 9(b). Similarly, the average monthly retail market price for one Kg of
beans decreased from KSh 169.3 in September 2023 to KSh 165.7 in October 2023, as out-
lined in Table 9(d).
The quantity of exported coffee decreased from 2,967.0 MT in September 2023 to 1,704.7 MT
in October 2023, similarly, its value decreased from KSh 2.0 billion to KSh 1.3 billion over the
same period. The quantity of exported tea decreased from 47,806.7 MT in September 2023 to
45,359.9 MT October 2023, while its value decreased from KSh 17.0 billion to KSh 16.1 billion
over the same period, as shown in Table 10.
Table 11 provides data on the monthly export of fresh horticultural produce. The quantity of ex-
ported cut flowers increased from 9,782.4 MT in September 2023 to 12,066.3 MT in October
2023, with a corresponding rise in value from KSh 5,888.9 million to KSh 6,790.4 million. The
quantity of exported fruits declined from 13,609.0 MT in September 2023 to 8,252.3 MT in Oc-
tober 2023. The value of exported fruits decreased from KSh 2,302.5 million to KSh 1,349.4
million during the same period. The quantity of exported vegetables amounted to 8,240.8 MT,
with a corresponding value of KSh 2,398.7 million, in October 2023.

In October 2023, Uganda and Pakistan emerged as Kenya's major trading partners for exports,
with values of KSh 9.0 billion and KSh 6.6 billion, respectively, as detailed in Table 13(a). Food

the total exports, while industrial supplies made up 24.4 per cent, as indicated in Table 13(b).
The main sources of imports, as outlined in Table 14(a), indicates that China was the primary
importing partner with a value of KSh 43.6 billion, followed by United Arab Emirates with a value
of KSh 41.9 billion in October 2023. The leading import category was non-food industrial sup-
plies, accounting for 32.0 per cent of total imports in October 2023. Food and beverages repre-
sented 12.2 per cent of the total imports during the same period, as presented in Table 14(b).

billion in September 2023, as highlighted in Table 14(c). The value of imports for telecommuni-
cation equipment rose from KSh 2.8 billion in August 2023 to KSh 3.2 billion in September
2023, as shown in Table 14(d).

Wind and Solar generation experienced growth, with wind generation increasing from 141.3
million KWh in September 2023 to 183.4 million KWh in October 2023, while solar generation
rose from 39.3 million KWh to 42.9 million KWh during the same period. However, hydro gener-
ation decreased from 247.2 million KWh in September 2023 to 205.3 million KWh in October
2023. Similarly, Geo-Thermal generation decreased from 511.9 million KWh to 509.5 million
KWh, during the same period. The volume of electricity exports declined from 4.9 million KWh
in September 2023 to 2.1 million KWh in October 2023. Conversely, electricity imports in-
creased from 49.9 million KWh in September to 67.0 million KWh in October 2023.
The national average domestic retail prices for motor gasoline premium, light diesel, and illumi-
nating kerosene were KSh 217.97, KSh 206.21 and KSh 205.79 per litre, respectively in Octo-
ber 2023. Charcoal prices averaged KSh 72.56 per Kg, while a 13-Kg cylinder of gas averaged
KSh 3,000.04 during the same period, as presented in Table 15(e). On the international mar-
ket, the OPEC crude oil basket price decreased from US Dollars 94.60 per barrel in September
2023 to US Dollars 91.78 per barrel in October 2023, as presented in Table 15(f).
Tables 17(a) to 17(f) outline various monthly manufacturing statistics. Sugar production in-
creased from 16,760 MT in September 2023 to 22,806 MT in October 2023. Production of soft
drinks increased from 38,269 thousand litres in May 2023 to 42,192 thousand litres in June
2023. The number of assembled vehicles increased from 906 units in June 2023 to 974 units in
July 2023. Production of galvanized sheets rose from 21,100 MT in June 2023 to 23,930 MT in
July 2023. Cement production decreased from 865,310 MT in August 2023 to 831,534 MT in
September 2023. Similarly, cement consumption decreased, from 826,947 MT to 799,297 MT
during the same period. Milk intakes in the formal sector rose from 67.7 million litres in Septem-
ber 2023 to 74.7 million litres in October 2023.

Tables 19(a) and 19(b) presents international passengers air traffic departures and arrivals
through Jomo Kenyatta International Airport. The number of international passengers depar-
tures decreased from 282,865 in September to 278,737 in October 2023. Conversely, interna-
tional passengers arrivals increased from 271,492 in September to 275,423 in October 2023.
Table 19(c) presents the numbers of newly registered motor vehicles categorized by body type.
The number of newly registered motor vehicles declined by 2.7 per cent from 18,864 units in
August 2023 to 18,352 units in September 2023.Newly registered motorcycles increased from
4,025 units in August 2023 to 5,343 units in September 2023.
The number of passengers travelling by the Standard Gauge Railway (SGR) decreased from
278,929 in August 2023 to 206,090 in September 2023,with a corresponding decrease in reve-
nue from KSh 295.5 million to KSh 226.7 million, during the same period. Similarly, the volume
of cargo transported via SGR dropped from 580,882 tonnes in August 2023 to 579,202 tonnes
in September 2023.Consequently, there was a 10.2 per cent drop in revenue from KSh 1,677.1
million in August 2023 to KSh 1,506.3 million in September 2023 as presented in Table 19(d).
The number of passengers using the Metre Gauge Railway (MGR) decreased from 330,927 in
August 2023 to 314,523 in September 2023. The revenue generated from MGR passenger traf-
fic decreased from KSh 18.62 million to KSh 17.18 million during the same period, as outlined in
Table 19(e).
Table 19(f) presents Mombasa Port throughput. The volume of cargo handled increased from
2,762.5 thousand tonnes in September 2023 to 2,979.1 thousand tonnes in October 2023. Im-
ports volume increased by 8.3 per cent from 2,234.4 thousand tonnes in September 2023 to
2,420.9 thousand tonnes in October 2023. In contrast, the exports volume slightly declined by
0.2 per cent to 379.3 thousand tonnes in October 2023.Transhipments and restows increased
by 22.0 per cent and 2.6 per cent, respectively, during the same period.
