iiKEY HIGHLIGHTS
The Monthly Leading Economic Indicators report highlights changes in Consumer Price Indices,
inflation rates, interest rates and exchange rates. In addition, the report details changes in se-
lected indicators of international trade, agriculture, energy, manufacturing, building and con-
struction, tourism and transport.
MACROECONOMIC INDICATORS
The Consumer Price Index (CPI) increased from an index of 136.71 in October 2023 to an in-
dex of 137.03 in November 2023, as indicated in Table 1(a). The overall inflation, as measured
by the Consumer Price Indices, decreased from 6.9 per cent to 6.8 per cent during the same
period, as presented in Table 1(b).
Table 2 provides the mean monthly foreign exchange rates of the Kenyan Shilling against major
selected trading currencies. In November 2023, the Kenyan Shilling depreciated against all se-
lected major trading currencies.
The average yield rate for 91-day Treasury Bills, which is a benchmark for the general trend of
interest rates, rose from 14.96 per cent in October 2023 to 15.32 per cent in November
2023.The Central Bank of Kenya maintained its benchmark interest rate at 10.50 per cent in
November 2023, Interbank rates decreased from 12.70 per cent in October 2023 to 11.32 per
cent in November 2023, as presented in Table 3.
The Nairobi Securities Exchange (NSE) 20 share index increased from 1,461 points in October
2023 to 1,496 points in November 2023. The total number of shares traded declined from 329
million to 275 million during the same period. The number of equity transactions increased
from 20,127 in October 2023 to 21,618 in November 2023. The Equities Market turnover de-
creased by 23.7 per cent to KSh 3.7 billion in November 2023, compared to KSh 4.8 billion rec-
orded in the month of October 2023, as outlined in Table 4.
Tables 5(a) provides statistics on Money Supply. Broad money supply (M3), a key indicator for
monetary policy formulation, increased from KSh 5,774.6 billion in August 2023 to KSh 5,841.1
billion in September 2023. Foreign currency deposits rose from KSh 1,328.6 billion in August
2023 to KSh 1,399.1 billion in September 2023. Gross foreign exchange reserves increased
from KSh 1,115.5 billion in August 2023 to KSh 1,121.5 billion in September 2023. Similarly, the
value of Net Foreign Exchange liabilities increased from KSh 675.2 billion to KSh 711.2 billion
during the same period, as presented in Table 5(b).
3AGRICULTURE
The quantity of coffee sold increased from 154.2 MT in August 2023 to 339.9 MT in September
2023, as shown in Table 6. The production of tea rose from 45,577.7 MT in August 2023 to
48,497.3 MT in September 2023. During the nine-month period of 2023, the average auction
price for Kenya tea was USD 2.25 per Kg as shown in Table 7.
Cane deliveries rose from 183.0 thousand metric tonnes in September 2023 to 257.4 thousand
metric tonnes in October 2023, as presented in Table 8. The average monthly retail market
price for one Kg of maize dropped from KSh 69.2 in October 2023 to KSh 68.2 in November
2023, as shown in Table 9(b). Conversely , the average monthly retail market price for one Kg
of beans rose from KSh 164.0 in October 2023 to KSh 165.2 in November 2023, as outlined in
Table 9(d).
The quantity of exported coffee decreased from 1,704.7 MT in October 2023 to 1,694.6 MT in
November 2023, while its value increased from KSh 1.3 billion to KSh 1.4 billion over the same
period. The quantity of exported tea increased from 45,359.9 MT in October 2023 to 49,507.3
MT in November 2023, while its value increased from KSh 16.1 billion to KSh 17.8 billion over
the same period, as shown in Table 10.
Table 11 provides data on the monthly export of fresh horticultural produce. The quantity of
exported cut flowers increased from 9,782.4 MT in September 2023 to 12,066.3 MT in October
2023, with a corresponding rise in value from KSh 5,888.9 million to KSh 6,790.4 million. The
quantity of exported fruits declined from 13,609.0 MT in September 2023 to 8,252.3 MT in Oc-
tober 2023. The value of exported fruits decreased from KSh 2,302.5 million to KSh 1,349.4
million during the same period. The quantity of exported vegetables was 8,240.8 MT, with a
corresponding value of KSh 2,398.7 million, in October 2023.

In November 2023, Uganda and Pakistan emerged as Kenya's major trading partners for ex-
ports, with values of KSh 9.0 billion and KSh 8.1 billion, respectively, as detailed in Table 13(a).
Food and beverages constituted a significant portion of domestic exports, comprising 43.0 per
cent of the total exports, while industrial supplies constituted 28.9 per cent, as indicated in Ta-
ble 13(b).
The main sources of imports, as outlined in Table 14(a), indicates that China was the primary
importing partner with a value of KSh 45.7 billion, followed by United Arab Emirates with a val-
ue of KSh 31.7 billion in November 2023. The leading import category was non-food industrial
supplies, accounting for 31.2 per cent of total imports in November 2023. Food and beverages
represented 9.3 per cent of the total imports during the same period, as presented in Table 14
(b).
The number of mobile money agents decreased from 333,291 in October 2023 to 327,928 in
November 2023. During the same period, the number of mobile money subscriptions increased
from 76.0 million to 77.1 million. The number of mobile money transactions declined from
210.6 million in October 2023 to 201.3 million in November 2023. However, the total value of
transactions rose from KSh 703.3 billion to KSh 707.6 billion during the same period, as high-
lighted in Table 14(c).

The total electricity generation and imports decreased from 1,148.6 million kWh in October
2023 to 1,113.7 million kWh in November 2023. Total KPLC sales decreased from 877.5 million
kWh to 862.2 million kWh during the same period. The total electricity imports experienced a
slight upswing from 67.0 million kWh in October 2023 to 68.1 million kWh in November 2023
while the total electricity exports was 2.8 million kWh in November 2023.
The national average domestic retail prices for motor gasoline premium, light diesel, and illumi-
nating kerosene were KSh 217.97, KSh 204.21 and KSh 203.79 per litre, respectively in No-
vember 2023. Charcoal prices averaged KSh 73.83 per Kg, while a 13-Kg cylinder of gas aver-
aged KSh 3,031.82 during the same period, as presented in Table 15(e). On the international
market, the OPEC crude oil basket price decreased from US Dollars 91.78 per barrel in October
2023 to US Dollars 84.92 per barrel in November 2023, as presented in Table 15(f).
The total value of buildings approved by Nairobi City County declined from KSh 18.0 billion in
October 2023 to KSh 16.2 billion in November 2023. The value of residential buildings ap-
proved increased from KSh 13.7 billion in October 2023 to KSh 14.0 billion in November 2023.
The value of non-residential buildings approved dropped from KSh 4.4 billion in October 2023
to KSh 2.2 billion in November 2023,as outlined in Table 16.
Tables 17(a) to 17(f) outline various monthly manufacturing statistics. Sugar production in-
creased from 16,760 MT in September 2023 to 22,806 MT in October 2023. Cement production
decreased from 865,310 MT in August 2023 to 831,534 MT in September 2023. Similarly, ce-
ment consumption decreased, from 826,947 MT to 799,297 MT during the same period. Milk
intakes in the formal sector declined from 74.7 million litres in October 2023 to 74.3 million li-
tres in November 2023.

Tables 19(a) and 19(b) presents international passengers air traffic departures and arrivals
through Jomo Kenyatta International Airport. The number of international passengers depar-
tures decreased from 282,865 in September to 278,737 in October 2023. Conversely, interna-
tional passengers arrivals increased from 271,492 in September to 275,423 in October 2023.
Table 19(c) presents the numbers of newly registered motor vehicles, categorized by body
type. The number of newly registered motor vehicles dropped from 18,352 units in September
2023 to 13,694 units in October 2023. Newly registered motorcycles decreased from 5,343
units in September 2023 to 4,690 units in October 2023.
The number of passengers travelling by the Standard Gauge Railway (SGR) decreased from
278,929 in August 2023 to 206,090 in September 2023,with a corresponding decrease in reve-
nue from KSh 295.5 million to KSh 226.7 million, during the same period. Similarly, the volume
of cargo transported via SGR dropped from 580,882 tonnes in August 2023 to 579,202 tonnes
in September 2023. Consequently, there was a 10.2 per cent drop in revenue from KSh 1,677.1
million in August 2023 to KSh 1,506.3 million in September 2023 as presented in Table 19(d).
The number of passengers using the Metre Gauge Railway (MGR) decreased from 330,927 in
August 2023 to 314,523 in September 2023. The revenue generated from MGR passenger traf-
fic decreased from KSh 18.62 million to KSh 17.18 million during the same period, as outlined in
Table 19(e).
Table 19(f) presents Mombasa Port throughput. The volume of cargo handled increased from
2,762.5 thousand tonnes in September 2023 to 2,979.1 thousand tonnes in October 2023. Im-
ports volume increased by 8.3 per cent from 2,234.4 thousand tonnes in September 2023 to
2,420.9 thousand tonnes in October 2023. In contrast, the exports volume slightly declined by
0.2 per cent to 379.3 thousand tonnes in October 2023. Transhipments and restows increased
by 22.0 per cent and 2.6 per cent, respectively, during the same period.
