iiKEY HIGHLIGHTS
The Monthly Leading Economic Indicators report highlights changes in Consumer Price Indices,
inflation rates, interest rates and exchange rates. In addition, the report details changes in se-
lected indicators of international trade, agriculture, energy, manufacturing, construction, tourism
and transport.
MACROECONOMIC INDICATORS
The Consumer Price Index (CPI) increased from an index of 138.15 in January 2024 to an index
of 138.34 in February 2024, a change of 0.1 per cent, as indicated in Table 1(a). The overall in-
flation rate, as measured by the Consumer Price Indices, was 6.3 per cent in February 2024, as
presented in Table 1(b).
Table 2 presents the mean monthly foreign exchange rates of the Kenyan Shilling against major
selected trading currencies. In February 2024, the Kenyan Shilling appreciated against all major
selected trading currencies. The average yield rate for 91-day Treasury Bills, which is a bench-
mark for interest rate trends, rose from 16.14 per cent in January 2024 to 16.51 per cent in Feb-
ruary 2024. The Central Bank of Kenya raised its benchmark interest rate to 13.0 in February
2024 from 12.50 per cent in January 2024. Interbank rates dropped from 13.65 per cent to
13.59 per cent during the same period, as presented in Table 3.
The Nairobi Securities Exchange (NSE) 20 share index grew from 1,509 points in January 2024 to
1,536 points in February 2024. During the same period, the total volume of shares traded rose
from 152 million to 276 million. The number of Equity transactions rose from 19,180 in January
2024 to 20,275 in February 2024. Notably, the Equities Market turnover surged by 71.5 per cent
to KSh 4.6 billion in February 2024, compared to KSh 2.6 billion recorded in January 2024, as
detailed in Table 4.
Tables 5(a) and 5(b) provides statistics on Money Supply. Broad money supply (M3), a significant
gauge for formulating monetary policies, declined from KSh 6,111.1 billion in January 2024 to
KSh 6,006.4 billion in February 2024. Similarly, foreign currency deposits declined from KSh
1,603.2 billion to KSh 1,504.1 billion, during the same period. The value of Gross Foreign Ex-
change Reserves decreased from KSh 2,431.0 billion in January 2024 to KSh 2,391.6 billion in
February 2024. Net Foreign Exchange Liabilities increased from KSh 857.8 billion to KSh 886.5
billion during the same period.

to KSh 1.4 billion.
4INTERNATIONAL TRADE AND ICT
Table 12 presents monthly statistics on
external trade. The volume of trade de-
creased from KSh 336.2 billion in Janu-
ary 2024 to KSh 333.8 billion in February
2024. The total value of exports rose
from KSh 91.7 billion to KSh 107.4 bil-
lion. The total value of imports de-
creased from KSh 244.6 billion to KSh
226.4 billion over the same period.
In February 2024, Uganda and Netherlands emerged as Kenya's major trading partners for ex-
ports with values of KSh 11.8 billion and KSh 7.5 billion respectively, as detailed in Table 13(a).
Food and beverages was the leading category of exports with 44.3 per cent, while non-food in-
dustrial supplies constituted 24.7 per cent of the total exports, as indicated in Table 13(b).
Table 14(a) outlines the main origins of imports. China was the leading importing partner with a
value of KSh 45.4 billion, followed by India with a value of KSh 25.2 billion in February 2024. The
leading imports category was non-food industrial supplies, accounting for 33.6 per cent of the
total imports in February 2024. Fuel and lubricants constituted 27.3 per cent of the total imports,
while Food and beverages constituted 8.7 per cent of the total imports during the same period,
as presented in Table 14(b).
The mobile money market saw slight decrease across all metrics from December 2023 to January
2024. The number of Mobile Money Agents decreased from 322,404 in December 2023 to
321,340 in January 2024. Mobile Money Subscriptions declined from 77.26 million to 76.76 mil-
lion, during the same period. The total number of transactions decreased from 213.31 million in
December 2023 to 211.58 million in January 2024. Similarly, the total value of transactions de-
creased from KSh 788.35 billion to KSh 783.98 billion during the same period, as detailed in Ta-
ble 14(c). The import value of telecommunication equipment rose from KSh 3.0 billion in January
2024 to KSh 3.6 billion in February 2024. Similarly, the import value of monitors, projectors, and
television reception apparatus increased significantly from KSh 0.3 billion to KSh 0.6 billion during
the same period. Conversely, the import value of automatic data processing machines and stor-
age units declined from KSh 1.9 billion to KSh 1.4 billion, as outlined in Table 14(d).
5ENERGY, CONSTRUCTION AND MANUFACTURING
Tables 15(a) and 15(b) presents
monthly electricity statistics. Local
electricity generation declined from
1,026.5 million KWh in January 2024
to 979.0 million KWh in February 2024.
Hydroelectric generation increased
from 254.51 million KWh in January
2024 to 261.66 million KWh in February 2024, while wind power rose notably from 136.13 million
KWh to 156.14 million KWh. Conversely, thermal generation dropped significantly from 90.65
million KWh to 74.57 million KWh. Total electricity generation and imports decreased from
1,168.7 million KWh to 1,110.4 million KWh. Additionally, total sales by KPLC dropped from 881.4
million KWh in January 2024 to 854.0 million KWh in February 2024, with total electricity imports
also declining from 142.3 million KWh to 131.5 million KWh, during the same period.
The national average retail prices for motor gasoline premium, light diesel, and illuminating kero-
sene were KSh 206.97, KSh 196.21, and KSh 193.96 per litre, respectively, in February 2024.
Charcoal prices averaged KSh 77.46 per kg, while a 13-kg cylinder of gas averaged KSh
3,187.10, during the same period, as indicated in Table 15(e). On the international market, the
OPEC crude oil basket price increased from US Dollars 80.04 per barrel in January 2024 to US
Dollars 81.23 per barrel in February 2024.
The total value of buildings approved by Nairobi City County rose from KSh 14.0 billion in Janu-
ary 2024 to KSh 16.2 billion in February 2024. The value of residential buildings approved in-
creased from KSh 11.6 billion in January 2024 to KSh 12.6 billion in February 2024. Similarly, the
value of non-residential buildings approved rose from KSh 2.4 billion to KSh 3.6 billion during the
same period, as outlined in Table 16.
Tables 17(a) to 17(f) outlines various monthly manufacturing statistics. Sugar production in-
creased from 60,680 MT in January 2024 to 63,075 MT in February 2024. Production of galva-
nized sheets rose from 23,847 MT in November 2023 to 24,178 MT in December 2023. In addi-
tions, production of soft drinks rose from 53,069 thousand litres in November 2023 to 78,974
thousand litres in December 2023. Milk intakes in the formal sector dropped from 74.6 million
litres in January 2024 to 71.8 million litres in February 2024.
6TOURISM AND TRANSPORT
The total number of arrivals through
both Jomo Kenyatta (JKIA) and Moi
International Airports (MIA) dropped
from 143,942 persons in December
2023 to 134,665 persons in January
2024, as indicated in Table 18.
Tables 19(a) and 19(b) presents international passengers' air traffic departures and arrivals
through Jomo Kenyatta International Airport. The number of international passenger departures
rose from 282,450 in December 2023 to 282,807 in January 2024. In contrast, international pas-
senger arrivals declined from 306,576 to 269,003 during the same period. The number of newly
registered motor vehicles, classified by body type, declined from 12,217 units in November 2023
to 10,349 units in December 2023. Similarly, the number of newly registered motorcycles de-
creased from 3,472 units to 3,223 units over the same period, as indicated in Table 19(c).
The number of passengers traveling by the Standard Gauge Railway (SGR) rose by 27.2 per cent
from 239,411 in November 2023 to 304,605 in December 2023, accompanied by a corresponding
revenue increase of 21.9 per cent from KSh 250.5 million to KSh 305.3 million. Cargo volume
transported via SGR rose from 514,843 tonnes to 520,313 tonnes, generating KSh 1.3 billion in
revenue during the same period, as presented in Table 19(d). Metre Gauge Railway (MGR) pas-
senger usage declined from 316,075 in November 2023 to 250,792 in December 2023. However,
revenue generated from MGR passenger traffic rose from KSh 18.6 million to KSh 27.5 million
during the same period, as indicated in Table 19(e).
Table 19(f) presents Mombasa Port throughput. The volume of cargo handled decreased from
3,006.2 thousand tonnes in January 2024 to 2,741.9 thousand tonnes in February 2024. Imports
volume decreased from 2,189.6 thousand tonnes in January 2024 to 1,877.6 thousand tonnes in
February 2024. In contrast, the exports volume increased from 351.4 thousand tonnes to 371.4
thousand tonnes. Transhipment rose by 4.0 per cent to 457.3 thousand tonnes while restows
rose by 39.7 per cent to 35.6 thousand tonnes, during the same period.
