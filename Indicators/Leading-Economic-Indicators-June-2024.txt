iiKEY HIGHLIGHTS
The Monthly Leading Economic Indicators report highlights changes in Consumer Price Indices,
inflation rates, interest rates and exchange rates. In addition, the report details changes in se-
lected indicators of international trade, agriculture, energy, manufacturing, construction, tourism
and transport.
MACROECONOMIC INDICATORS
The Consumer Price Index (CPI) increased from an index of 139.64 in May 2024 to an index of
140.23 in June 2024, as shown in Table 1(a). The overall year on year inflation rate as measured
by the Consumer Price Index (CPI) was 4.6 per cent, in June 2024, as presented in Table 1(b).
The Kenyan Shilling appreciated against all major selected trading currencies in June 2024, as
outlined in Table 2. The average yield rate for 91-day Treasury Bills, which is a benchmark for
interest rate trends, rose from 15.91 per cent in May 2024 to 15.97 per cent in June 2024. The
Central Bank of Kenya maintained its benchmark interest rate at 13.0 per cent in June 2024. In-
terbank rates dropped from 13.62 per cent in May 2024 to 13.14 per cent in June 2024, as pre-
sented in Table 3.
The NSE 20 Share index fell from 1,722 points in May 2024 to 1,657 points in June 2024. The
total number of shares traded dropped from 510 million to 279 million. The total number of equi-
ty transactions decreased from 27,334 to 23,076 during the same period. The Equities turnover
decreased from KSh 16.0 billion in May 2024 to KSh 5.0 billion June 2024, as outlined in Table 4.
The money supply (M2) increased from KSh 4,464.5 billion in April 2024 to KSh 4,525.7 billion in
May 2024. Similarly, the broad money supply (M3) grew by 0.24 per cent, rising from KSh
5,836.3 billion in April 2024 to KSh 5,850.1 billion in May 2024. Foreign currency deposits de-
creased from KSh 1,371.8 billion to KSh 1,324.4 billion during the same period, as presented in
Table 5(a). The value of Gross Foreign Exchange Reserves decreased from KSh 2,096.5 billion in
April 2024 to KSh 2,016.3 billion in May 2024. Net Foreign Exchange Liabilities decreased from
KSh 824.2 billion to KSh 782.3 billion during the same period, as shown in Table 5(b).
1AGRICULTURE
The quantity of coffee auctioned at the
Nairobi Coffee Exchange rose from 4.8
thousand MT in February 2024 to 5.1
thousand MT in March 2024. The price of
coffee auctioned dropped from US Dollars
5.09 per Kg to US Dollars 4.84 per Kg,
during the same period, as presented in Table 6.
Tea production decreased from 54.3 thousand MT in March 2024, to 53.9 thousand MT in April
2024. The average auction price for Kenya tea was higher at US Dollars 2.29 per Kg in April 2024
compared to US Dollars 2.15 per Kg in March 2024. The average auction price for Kenya tea for
the four months period of the year 2024 stood at US Dollars 2.22 per Kg, as shown in Table 7.
Cane deliveries rose by 10.4 per cent from 756.0 thousand MT in February 2024 to 834.9 thou-
sand MT in March 2024, as shown in Table 8. Tables 9(b) and 9(d) present the average retail
market prices for one Kg of maize and one Kg of beans, respectively. The average monthly retail
market price for one Kg of maize decreased from KSh 52.2 in May 2024 to KSh 51.0 in June
2024. Similarly, the average monthly retail market price for one Kg of beans decreased from KSh
153.9 to KSh 152.4, during the same period.
The quantity of coffee exported rose from 4.7 thousand MT in May 2024 to 5.8 thousand MT in
June 2024, with a corresponding increase in value from KSh 3.6 billion to KSh 4.1 billion, during
the same period. The quantity of tea exported decreased from 51.6 thousand MT in May 2024 to
42.9 thousand MT in June 2024. In addition, its value dropped from KSh 15.6 billion to KSh 11.8
billion during the same period, as outlined in Table 10.
The volume of exported cut flowers fell from 16.1 thousand MT in February 2024 to 8.7 thousand
MT in March 2024, along with a decrease in value from KSh 13.3 billion to KSh 6.1 billion. The
volume of exported fruits more than doubled, rising from 5.0 thousand MT in February 2024 to
12.3 thousand MT in March 2024, with a corresponding increase in value from KSh 1.0 billion to
KSh 2.0 billion. The volume of exported vegetables dropped from 16.2 thousand MT in February
2024 to 14.6 thousand MT in March 2024, with a corresponding decrease in value from KSh 3.6
billion to KSh 2.8 billion, as shown in Table 11.
2INTERNATIONAL TRADE AND ICT
The volume of trade decreased
from KSh 307.1 billion in May
2024 to KSh 295.8 billion in June
2024. The total export value fell
from KSh 94.5 billion to KSh 87.2
billion during the same period.
The total import value decreased
from KSh 212.6 billion in May
2024 to KSh 208.6 billion in June
2024, as presented in Table 12.
Uganda and USA were Kenya's primary export destinations, with values of KSh 10.1 billion and
KSh 6.1 billion, respectively, in June 2024, as outlined in Table 13(a). Food and beverages con-
stituted the largest category of exports, accounting for 41.2 per cent, while non-food industrial
supplies made up 29.3 per cent of the total exports in June 2024, as presented in Table 13(b).
China and the United Arab Emirates were the leading importing partners with values of KSh 42.1
billion and KSh 29.5 billion respectively, in June 2024, as outlined in Table 14(a). The dominant
import category was non-food industrial supplies, constituting 34.9 per cent of the total imports.
Fuel and lubricants accounted for 27.0 per cent, while food and beverages accounted for 10.9
per cent of the total imports in June 2024, as shown in Table 14(b).
The number of active mobile money agents decreased from 334.1 thousand in May 2024 to
334.0 thousand in June 2024. The number of mobile money subscriptions increased from 77.2
million to 77.9 million. The total number of transactions dropped from 208.1 million to 201.1 mil-
lion, during the same period. The total value of transactions decreased from KSh 722.3 billion in
May 2024 to KSh 676.0 billion in June 2024, as shown in Table 14(c). The export value of tele-
communications equipment decreased from KSh 88.8 million in May 2024 to KSh 48.2 million in
June 2024. Additionally, their import value decreased from KSh 3.7 billion to KSh 3.2 billion dur-
ing the same period, as presented in Table 14(d).
3ENERGY, CONSTRUCTION AND MANUFACTURING
Local electricity generation decreased
from 1,023.5 million KWh in May 2024 to
1,020.2 million KWh in June 2024, as de-
tailed in Table 15(a). Total electricity gen-
eration and import dropped from 1,152.4
million KWh in May 2024 to 1,138.6 million KWh in June 2024. Total sales by KPLC dropped from
892.4 million KWh to 875.3 million KWh. Electricity import decreased from 128.9 million KWh to
118.4 million KWh. Electricity export increased from 3.9 million KWh to 4.0 million KWh during
the same period, as shown in Table 15(b).
Table 15(c) presents the consumption volumes for various petroleum fuels. The consumption of
AGO (Light Diesel Oil) decreased from 182.4 thousand MT in May 2024 to 165.8 thousand MT in
June 2024. Jet Fuel consumption increased from 59.8 thousand MT to 60.6 thousand MT. Motor
Spirit consumption decreased from 117.9 thousand MT to 106.8 thousand MT. Illuminating Kero-
sene consumption decreased from 3.2 thousand MT to 3.1 thousand MT. LPG consumption de-
creased from 36.2 thousand MT to 33.3 thousand MT, during the same period.
The national average retail prices for motor gasoline premium, light diesel, and illuminating kero-
sene stood at KSh 190.46, KSh 173.83, and KSh 163.82 per litre, respectively, in June 2024.
Charcoal prices averaged KSh 83.80 per kg, while a 13-kg cylinder of gas averaged KSh
3,213.97, during the same period, as shown in Table 15(e). On the international market, the
OPEC crude oil basket price decreased from US Dollars 83.59 per barrel in May 2024 to US Dol-
lars 83.22 per barrel in June 2024, as presented in Table 15(f).
The value of approved residential buildings by Nairobi City County increased from KSh 20.3 billion
in May 2024 to KSh 25.3 billion in June 2024. Similarly, the value of approved non-residential
buildings rose from KSh 3.4 billion to KSh 4.0 billion during the same period, as shown in Table
16.
Production of soft drinks increased from 53.3 million litres in April 2024 to 60.6 million litres in
May 2024, as presented in Table 17(b). Milk intake in the formal sector increased from 76.4 mil-
lion litres in May 2024 to 77.8 million litres in June 2024, as shown in Table 17(f).
4TOURISM AND TRANSPORT
The total number of arrivals at Jomo Ken-
yatta (JKIA) and Moi (MIA) International
Airports rose from 117,639 persons in May
2024 to 149,922 persons in June 2024. No-
tably, arrivals at JKIA increased from
111,925 persons to 140,617 persons during
the same period, as presented in Table 18.
Tables 19(a) and 19(b) present embarked passengers by port of destination and by port of origin,
through Jomo Kenyatta International Airport, respectively. The number of embarked passengers
by port of destination rose from 248,727 in April 2024 to 259,304 in May 2024. Similarly, the
number of passengers who landed by port of origin grew from 243,532 to 257,876 during the
same period. The total number of newly registered motor vehicles, categorized by body type,
dropped from 18,509 units in February 2024 to 15,984 units in March 2024. The number of newly
registered motorcycles dropped from 8,561 units to 6,509 units during the same period, as
shown in Table 19(c).
The number of passengers traveling by the Standard Gauge Railway (SGR) rose from 168,525 in
May 2024 to 186,920 in June 2024, with a corresponding increase in revenue from KSh 279.3
million to KSh 307.0 million. Cargo volume transported via SGR dropped from 615.6 thousand
tonnes to 562.4 thousand tonnes, with a drop in revenue from KSh 1.3 billion to KSh 1.0 billion
during the same period, as presented in Table 19(d). Metre Gauge Railway (MGR) passenger us-
age rose from 140,840 in May 2024 to 182,332 in June 2024. In addition, revenue generated
from MGR passenger traffic rose from KSh 7.4 million to KSh 9.5 million during the same period,
as presented in Table 19(e).
Table 19(f) presents Mombasa Port throughput. The volume of cargo handled grew from 3.4 mil-
lion MT in April 2024 to 3.6 million MT in May 2024. Import volume stood at 2.7 million MT in
May 2024. Export volume rose from 360.3 thousand MT in April 2024 to 423.9 thousand MT in
May 2024. Transshipment rose by 27.8 per cent to 468.8 thousand MT. Restows more than dou-
bled to 19.3 thousand MT in May from 9.4 thousand MT recorded in April 2024.
