2KEY HIGHLIGHTS
OVERVIEW
The Monthly Leading Economic Indicators report highlights changes in Consumer Price Indices (CPI) and in-
flation, interest & exchange rates. In addition, the report details changes in selected indicators of international
trade, agriculture, energy, manufacturing, building and construction, tourism and transport.
MACROECONOMIC INDICATORS
The Consumer Price Index (CPI) increased by 0.2 per cent from an index of 128.99 in December 2022 to
129.29 in January 2023. The overall rate of inflation dropped from 9.06 per cent to 8.98 per cent over the
same period. In January 2023, the Kenyan Shilling depreciated against all major trading currencies. The aver-
age yield rate for the 91-day Treasury bills, which is a benchmark for the general trend of interest rates rose
from 9.19 per cent in November 2022 to 9.33 per cent in December 2022.
The Nairobi Securities Exchange (NSE) 20 share index decreased from 1,676 points in December 2022 to
1,657 points in January 2023. During the same period, the total number of shares traded increased from 165
million to 333 million. The Equities Market turnover increased by 58.2 % to Ksh 7.81 billion in January 2023
compared to Ksh 4.94 billion recorded in the month of December 2022.
Broad money supply (M3), a key indicator for monetary policy formulation, increased from KSh 4,460.19 bil-
lion in November 2022 to KSh 4,534.50 billion in December 2022. The value of Gross Foreign Exchange Re-
serves increased from KSh 1,392.57 billion in November 2022 to KSh 1,494.20 billion in December 2022.
During the same period, the value of Net Foreign Exchange Reserves rose from KSh 278.29 billion to KSh
285.35 billion.

3Volume of trade dropped from KSh 268.98 billion in December 2022 to KSh 267.48 billion in January 2023.

mestic exports, while non-food industrial supplies accounted for 32.43 per cent of the domestic exports.
Quantity of coffee exported decreased from 2,223.80 MT in December 2022 to 1,921.19 MT in January 2023
while its value dropped from KSh 1,415.65 million to KSh 1,217.32 million over the same period. The quantity
of tea exported decreased from 47,847.99 MT in December 2022 to 44,556.08 MT in January 2023. The val-
ue of exported tea dropped from KSh 14,892.05 million to KSh 13,519.65 million over the same period.
Imports by BEC indicate that non-food industrial supplies were the main import category in December 2022
with a share of 40.34 per cent. Fuel and lubricants; Machinery & other capital equipment; and transport
equipment accounted for 21.88, 9.89 and 6.94 per cent of the total value of imports, respectively. Food and
beverages accounted for 12.41 per cent of the total imports in January 2023. .
