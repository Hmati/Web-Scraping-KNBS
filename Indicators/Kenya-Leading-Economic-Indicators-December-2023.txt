iiKEY HIGHLIGHTS
The Monthly Leading Economic Indicators report highlights changes in Consumer Price Indices,
inflation rates, interest rates and exchange rates. In addition, the report details changes in se-
lected indicators of international trade, agriculture, energy, manufacturing, construction, tourism
and transport.
MACROECONOMIC INDICATORS
The Consumer Price Index (CPI) increased by 0.4 per cent from an index of 137.03 in November
2023 to an index of 137.55 in December 2023, as indicated in Table 1(a). The overall inflation,
as measured by the Consumer Price Indices, decreased from 6.8 per cent to 6.6 per cent during
the same period, as presented in Table 1(b).
Table 2 presents the mean monthly foreign exchange rates of the Kenyan Shilling against major
selected trading currencies. In December 2023, the Kenyan Shilling depreciated against all major
selected trading currencies.
The average yield rate for 91-day Treasury Bills, which is a benchmark for the general trend of
interest rates, rose from 15.32 per cent in November 2023 to 15.70 per cent in December 2023.
The Central Bank of Kenya lifted its benchmark interest rate to 12.50 per cent in December 2023
from 10.50 in November 2023, as presented in Table 3.
The Nairobi Securities Exchange (NSE) 20 share index grew from 1,496 points in November 2023
to 1,501 points in December 2023. The total number of shares traded dropped from 275 million
to 208 million during the same period. The number of Equity transactions dropped from 21,618
in November 2023 to 15,329 in December 2023. Equities Market turnover declined to KSh 3.3
billion in December 2023, compared to the KSh 3.7 billion recorded in November 2023, as out-
lined in Table 4.
Tables 5(a) and 5(b) provides statistics on Money Supply. Broad money supply (M3), a key indi-
cator for monetary policy formulation, increased from KSh 5,841.1 billion in September 2023 to
KSh 5,882.7 billion in October 2023. Foreign currency deposits rose from KSh 1,399.1 billion in
September 2023 to KSh 1,450.9 billion in October 2023. In addition, the value of Net Foreign Ex-
change Liabilities increased from KSh 746.5 billion to KSh 774.5 billion during the same period.
3AGRICULTURE
The quantity of coffee sold increased from 154.2 MT in August 2023 to 339.9 MT in September
2023, as presented in Table 6. Tea production rose to 52,792.7 MT in October 2023 compared to
48,497.3 MT in September 2023. The average auction price for Kenya tea in October 2023 was
lower at USD 2.16 per Kg against USD 2.25 per Kg in September 2023. The average auction price
for Kenya tea for the ten-month period of 2023 was USD 2.24 per Kg, as shown in Table 7.
Cane deliveries more than doubled from 296.2 thousand metric tonnes in November 2023 to
610.0 thousand metric tonnes in December 2023, as presented in Table 8. Tables 9(b) and 9(d)
presents the average retail market prices for one Kg of both maize and beans. The average
monthly retail market price for one Kg of maize rose from KSh 69.2 in November 2023 to KSh
70.2 in December 2023. Conversely, the average monthly retail market price for one Kg of beans
declined from KSh 175.1 to KSh 170.3 during the same period.
The quantity of exported coffee declined from 1,694.6 MT in November 2023 to 1,478.3 MT in
December 2023, with a corresponding decrease in value from KSh 1.4 billion to KSh 1.2 billion.
The quantity of exported tea decreased from 49,507.3 MT in November 2023 to 46,524.9 MT in
December 2023. Additionally, its value dropped from KSh 17.8 billion to KSh 17.3 billion over the
same period, as outlined in Table 10.
Table 11 provides data on the monthly exports of fresh horticultural produce. The quantity of ex-
ported cut flowers declined from 12,841.9 MT in November 2023 to 4,684.6 MT in December
2023, accompanied by a corresponding decrease in value from KSh 9.5 billion to KSh 3.2 billion.
Similarly, the quantity of exported fruits decreased from 3,474.9 MT in November 2023 to
1,699.5 MT in December 2023, with a drop in value from KSh 0.6 billion to KSh 0.2 billion during
the same period. The quantity of exported vegetables decreased from 14,599.8 MT in November
2023 to 4,777.6 MT in December 2023, with a corresponding decline in value from KSh 7.5 billion
to KSh 1.4 billion.
4INTERNATIONAL TRADE AND ICT
Table 12 presents monthly statistics on
external trade. The volume of trade de-
clined from KSh 348.1 billion in Novem-
ber 2023 to KSh 303.9 billion in Decem-
ber 2023. Total exports value decreased
from KSh 89.0 billion to KSh 84.2 bil-
lion, while the total imports value de-
creased from KSh 259.1 billion to KSh
219.7 billion, during the same period.
In December 2023, Uganda and Pakistan emerged as Kenya's major trading partners for exports,
with values of KSh 8.8 billion and KSh 7.3 billion respectively, as detailed in Table 13(a). Food
and beverages constituted 43.4 per cent while Industrial Supplies (Non-Food) constituted 23.3
per cent of the total exports, during the same period, as indicated in Table 13(b).
Table 14(a) outlines the main origins of imports. China was the leading importing partner with a
value of KSh 44.7 billion, followed by the United Arab Emirates with a value of KSh 31.0 billion in
December 2023. The leading imports category was non-food industrial supplies, accounting for
35.3 per cent of the total imports in December 2023. Fuel and lubricants constituted 25.6 per-
cent of the total imports, while Food and beverages constituted 10.2 per cent of the total imports
during the same period, as presented in Table 14(b).
Table 14(c) highlights mobile money statistics. The number of mobile money agents decreased
from 327,928 in November 2023 to 322,404 in December 2023. The number of mobile money
subscriptions increased from 77.1 million to 77.3 million during the same period. The number of
mobile money transactions rose from 201.3 million in November 2023 to 213.3 million in Decem-
ber 2023. In addition, the total value of transactions rose from KSh 707.6 billion to KSh 788.4
billion during the same period. The value of imports for telecommunication equipment rose from
KSh 3.1 billion in October 2023 to KSh 3.4 billion in November 2023, as shown in Table 14(d).
5ENERGY, CONSTRUCTION AND MANUFACTURING
Tables 15(a) and 15(b) presents month-
ly electricity statistics. Local electricity
generation declined from 1,046.2 million
KWh in November 2023 to 970.8 million
KWh in December 2023. The total elec-
tricity generation and imports decreased
from 1,114.3 million KWh in November
2023 to 1,109.5 million KWh in December 2023. Total sales by KPLC dropped from 859.4 million
KWh to 828.3 million KWh during the same period. Notably, total electricity imports increased
from 68.1 million KWh in November 2023 to 138.7 million KWh in December 2023. In addition,
total electricity exports rose from 2.8 million KWh to 4.0 million KWh over the same period.
The national average domestic retail prices for motor gasoline premium, light diesel, and illumi-
nating kerosene were KSh 212.97, KSh 202.21, and KSh 199.78 per liter respectively, in Decem-
ber 2023. Charcoal prices averaged KSh 75.65 per Kg while a 13-Kg cylinder of gas averaged
KSh 3,032.62, during the same period as presented in Table 15(e). On the international market,
the OPEC crude oil basket price decreased from US Dollars 84.92 per barrel in November 2023 to
US Dollars 79.0 per barrel in December 2023.
The total value of buildings approved by Nairobi City County rose from KSh 16.2 billion in No-
vember 2023 to KSh 24.9 billion in December 2023. The value of residential buildings approved
increased from KSh 14.0 billion in November 2023 to KSh 21.4 billion in December 2023. Similar-
ly, the value of non-residential buildings approved rose from KSh 2.2 billion to KSh 3.5 billion
during the same period, as outlined in Table 16.
Tables 17(a) to 17(f) outlines various monthly manufacturing statistics. Sugar production in-
creased from 25,179 MT in November 2023 to 48,877 MT in December 2023. Cement production
decreased from 865,310 MT in August 2023 to 831,534 MT in September 2023. Similarly, cement
consumption decreased from 826,947 MT to 799,297 MT during the same period. Milk intakes in
the formal sector rose from 74.3 million liters in November 2023 to 75.7 million liters in Decem-
ber 2023.
6TOURISM AND TRANSPORT
The total number of visitors arriving
through Jomo Kenyatta (JKIA) and Moi
International Airports (MIA) decreased
from 131,756 persons in October 2023
to 123,604 persons in November 2023,
as shown in Table 18.
Tables 19(a) and 19(b) presents international passengers' air traffic departures and arrivals
through Jomo Kenyatta International Airport. The number of international passengers' departures
increased from 268,913 in November 2023 to 282,450 in December 2023. In addition, the num-
ber of international passengers' arrivals increased from 263,685 to 306,576, during the same pe-
riod.
The numbers of newly registered motor vehicles, categorized by body type, decreased from
18,352 units in September 2023 to 13,694 units in October 2023. Newly registered motorcycles
decreased from 5,343 units in September 2023 to 4,690 units in October 2023, as outlined in Ta-
ble 19(c).
The number of passengers traveling by the Standard Gauge Railway (SGR) decreased from
278,929 in August 2023 to 206,090 in September 2023, with a corresponding decrease in reve-
nue from KSh 295.5 million to KSh 226.7 million during the same period. Similarly, the volume of
cargo transported via SGR dropped from 580,882 tonnes in August 2023 to 579,202 tonnes in
September 2023. Consequently, revenue dropped from KSh 1.7 billion in August 2023 to KSh 1.5
billion in September 2023, as presented in Table 19(d). The number of passengers using the Me-
tre Gauge Railway (MGR) decreased from 330,927 in August 2023 to 314,523 in September 2023.
The revenue generated from MGR passenger traffic decreased from KSh 18.6 million to KSh 17.2
million during the same period, as outlined in Table 19(e).
Table 19(f) presents Mombasa Port throughput. The volume of cargo handled decreased from
3,286.7 thousand tonnes in November 2023 to 3,000.3 thousand tonnes in December 2023. Im-
ports volume decreased from 2,533.8 thousand tonnes in November 2023 to 2,191.4 thousand
tonnes in December 2023. In contrast, the exports volume increased from 367.8 thousand tonnes
to 403.7 thousand tonnes, during the same period.
