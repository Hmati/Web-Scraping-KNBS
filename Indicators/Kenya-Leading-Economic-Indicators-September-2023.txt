iiKEY HIGHLIGHTS
The Monthly Leading Economic Indicators report highlights changes in Consumer Price Indices,
inflation, interest rates and exchange rates. In addition, the report details changes in selected
indicators of international trade, agriculture, energy, manufacturing, building and construction,
tourism and transport.
MACROECONOMIC INDICATORS
The Consumer Price Index (CPI) increased by 1.0 per cent, from an index of 134.02 in August
2023 to an index of 135.32 in September 2023, as indicated in Table 1(a). Additionally, the over-
all inflation, as measured by the Consumer Price Indices, increased from 6.7 per cent to 6.8 per
cent during the same period, as presented in Table 1(b).
In September 2023, the Kenyan shilling demonstrated varied performance against selected ma-
jor trading currencies. The shilling appreciated against the Sterling Pound and the Euro, but de-
preciated against the US Dollar, Japanese Yen, and South African Rand, as shown in Table 2.
The average yield for 91-day Treasury Bills, a significant indicator of interest rate trends, in-
creased from 13.25 per cent in August 2023 to 14.38 per cent in September 2023. The central
bank of Kenya maintained its benchmark interest rate at 10.50 per cent in September 2023. Av-
erage deposit rates rose from 8.10 per cent in July 2023 to 8.39 per cent in August 2023. Over-
draft rates followed suit, increasing from 12.84 per cent to 13.24 per cent during the same peri-
od. The Inter-bank rates went up significantly from 10.34 per cent in July 2023 to 12.57 per cent
in August 2023. Additionally, savings rates (Commercial Banks Rates) exhibited a modest in-
crease, from 3.97 per cent to 4.05 per cent over the same period, as shown in Table 3.
The Nairobi Securities Exchange (NSE) 20 share index declined from 1,540 points in August
2023 to 1,508 points in September 2023. During the same period, the total volume of shares
traded increased from 241 million to 424 million. The number of equity transactions decreased
from 23,885 in August to 20,474 in September 2023. Equities Market turnover increased from
KSh 4.38 billion to KSh 5.22 billion during the same period, as outlined in Table 4.
Table 5(a) provides statistics on Money Supply. Broad money supply (M3), a crucial indicator for
monetary policy, increased from KSh 5,698.19 billion in July 2023 to KSh 5,811.98 billion in Au-
gust 2023. Foreign currency deposits rose from KSh 1,248.37 billion in July 2023 to KSh
1,328.58 billion in August 2023, as presented in Table 5(b).
3AGRICULTURE
The quantity of coffee sold dropped from 4,407.45 MT in April 2023 to 1,373.50 MT in May
2023, as indicated in Table 6. The production of tea decreased from 48,127.89 MT in June
2023 to 44,696.62 MT in July 2023.The average auction price for Kenya tea in July 2023 was
slightly lower at KSh 291.30 per Kg compared to KSh 291.87 per Kg in June 2023,while its av-
erage auction price during the seven-month period of 2023 stood at 2.25 USD per Kg as shown
in Table 7.
Cane deliveries declined from 395.23 thousand metric tonnes in July 2023 to 295.81 thousand
metric tonnes in August 2023 as shown in Table 8. The average monthly retail market price for
one Kg of maize decreased from KSh 75.70 in August 2023 to KSh 71.20 in September
2023,as shown in Table 9(b).On the other hand, the average monthly retail market price for one
Kg of beans increased from KSh 168.40 in August 2023 to KSh 170.40 in September 2023,as
outlined in Table 9(d).
The quantity of exported coffee decreased from 4,652.03 MT in August 2023 to 2,967.04 MT in
September 2023, along with a decrease in value from KSh 3.26 billion to KSh 2.02 billion over
the same period. The quantity of exported tea decreased from 58,590.66 MT in August 2023 to
47,806.68 MT September 2023, while the value decreased from KSh 20.19 billion to KSh 16.98
billion over the same period, as shown in Table 10.
Table 11 presents data on the monthly export of fresh horticultural produce. The quantity of ex-
ported vegetables increased from 5,254.93 MT in July 2023 to 6,536.28 MT in August 2023,
with a rise in value from KSh 1,565.52 million to KSh 1,960.67 million during the same period.
Additionally, the quantity of exported fruits increased from 22,916.07 MT in July 2023 to
23,159.46 MT in August 2023, with their value rising from KSh 3,702.47 million to KSh 3,880.63
million. The quantity of cut flowers exported was 9,121.84 MT, with a corresponding value of
KSh 5,530.50 million in August 2023.

In September 2023, Uganda and Pakistan emerged as Kenya's major trading partners for ex-
ports, with values of KSh 10.10 billion and KSh 8.38 billion, respectively, as detailed in Table 13

per cent of the total exports, while industrial supplies made up 26.4 per cent, as indicated in Ta-
ble 13(b). The main sources of imports, as outlined in Table 14(a), indicate that United Arab
Emirates was the primary importing partner with a value of KSh 41.59 billion, followed by China
with a value of KSh 40.96 billion in September 2023. The leading import category was non-food
industrial supplies, accounting for 34.6 per cent of total imports in September 2023. Food and
beverages represented 12.4 per cent of the total imports during the same period, as presented
in Table 14(b).
The number of mobile money agents increased from 330,912 in July 2023 to 333,428 in August

in July 2023 to KSh 208.61 million in August 2023. However, the total value of mobile money

2023,as highlighted in Table 14(c). The value of imports for telecommunication equipment de-
creased from KSh 4.49 billion in July 2023 to KSh 2.80 billion in August 2023,as shown in Table

Electricity exports, saw an increase of 10.6 per cent, from 4.45 million KWh in August 2023 to
4.92 million KWh in September 2023. In contrast, electricity imports decreased from 55.47 mil-
lion KWh in August to 49.93 million KWh in September 2023.Geo-Thermal emerged as the

247.20 million KWh in September 2023.
The national average domestic retail prices for motor gasoline premium, light diesel, and kero-
sene were KSh 212.25, KSh 201.73 and KSh 203.34 per litre, respectively in September 2023,
as presented in Table 15(e). Charcoal prices averaged KSh 71.66 per Kg, while a 13-Kg cylin-
der of gas averaged KSh 2,795.69 during the same period. On the international front, the
OPEC crude oil basket price increased from US Dollars 87.33 per barrel in August 2023 to US
Dollars 94.01 per barrel in September 2023,as presented in Table 15(f).
Tables 17(a) to 17(f) outline various monthly manufacturing metrics. Sugar production wit-
nessed a notable decline from 33,246 MT in July 2023 to 27,680 MT in August 2023. Produc-
tion of soft drinks increased from 38,269 thousand litres in May 2023 to 42,192 thousand litres
in June 2023. The number of assembled vehicles increased from 906 units in June 2023 to 974
units in July 2023. Production of galvanized sheets rose from 21,100 MT in June 2023 to
23,930 MT in July 2023. Cement production decreased from 865,310 MT in August 2023 to
831,534 MT in September 2023. Similarly, cement consumption decreased, from 826,947 MT
to 799,297 MT during the same period. Milk intakes in the formal sector declined from 71.48
million litres in August to 67.71 million litres in September 2023.
6TOURISM AND TRANSPORT
The total number of visitors arriving
through Jomo Kenyatta (JKIA) and Moi
International Airports (MIA) decreased
from 158,100 persons in July 2023 to
158,093 persons in August 2023, as
shown in Table 18.
Tables 19(a) and 19(b) presents international passengers air traffic departures and arrivals
through Jomo Kenyatta International Airport. The number of international passengers depar-
tures increased by 4.9 per cent from 291,369 in July 2023 to 305,735 in August 2023. The num-
ber of international passengers arrivals, saw a slight increase of 0.8 per cent, from 299,108 in
July 2023 to 301,644 in August 2023.
Table 19(c) presents the numbers of newly registered motor vehicles categorized by body type.
The number of newly registered motor vehicles declined marginally by 2.7 per cent from 18,864
units in August 2023 to 18,352 units in September 2023. Newly registered motorcycles in-
creased from 4,025 units in August 2023 to 5,343 units in September 2023
The Standard Gauge Railway (SGR) witnessed a substantial 26.1 per cent decrease in passen-
ger numbers, declining from 278,929 in August 2023 to 206,090 in September 2023.This reduc-
tion in passengers led to a notable 23.3 per cent decrease in revenue from KSh 295.5 million in
August 2023 to KSh 226.7 million in September 2023. Similarly, the volume of cargo transport-
ed via SGR saw a slight dip from 580,882 tonnes in August 2023 to 579,202 thousand tonnes in
September 2023. Consequently, there was a 10.2 per cent drop in revenue from KSh 1,677.1
million in August 2023 to KSh 1,506.3 million in September 2023 as presented in Table 19(d).
The number of passengers using the Metre Gauge Railway (MGR) decreased from 330,927 in
August 2023 to 314,523 in September 2023. This decline in passengers resulted in a decrease
in revenue generated from MGR passenger traffic from KSh 18.62 million to KSh 17.18 million
during the same period, as outlined in Table 19(e).
Table 19(f) presents Mombasa Port throughput. The volume of cargo handled declined by 6.5
per cent from 2,955.36 thousand tonnes in August 2023 to 2,762.51 thousand tonnes in Sep-
tember 2023. Imports volume declined by 5.5 per cent from 2,363.28 thousand tonnes in August
2023 to 2,234.44 thousand in September 2023. Additionally, exports volume reduced by 9.8
per cent from 421.33 thousand tonnes in August 2023 to 379.99 thousand tonnes in September
2023. Transshipment and restows declined by 12.9 and 19.5 per cent respectively, during the
same period.
