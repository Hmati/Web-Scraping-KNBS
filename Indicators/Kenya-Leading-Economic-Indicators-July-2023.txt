iiKEY HIGHLIGHTS
The Monthly Leading Economic Indicators report highlights changes in Consumer Price Indices, inflation,
interest rates and exchange rates. In addition, the report details changes in selected indicators of interna-
tional trade, agriculture, energy, manufacturing, building and construction, tourism and transport.
MACROECONOMIC INDICATORS
The Consumer Price Index (CPI) increased by 0.1 per cent from an index of 134.01 in June 2023
to 134.15 in July 2023 as presented in Table 1(a). The overall inflation as measured by the Con-
sumer Price Indices decreased from 7.9 per cent to 7.3 per cent over the same period as shown
in Table 1(b).
Table 2 presents mean monthly foreign exchange rates of Kenyan Shilling against selected major
trading currencies. In July 2023, the Kenyan Shilling depreciated against all major trading curren-
cies except against the Tanzanian Shilling.
The average yield rate for the 91-day Treasury Bills, which is a benchmark for the general trend
of interest rates rose from 11.49 per cent in June 2023 to 12.12 per cent in July 2023. During the
same period the average deposit rate increased from 7.70 per cent to 10.24 per cent. The Cen-
tral Bank Rate (CBR) was 10.50 in July 2023.The Interbank rates rose from 9.37 per cent in May
2023 to 9.55 per cent in June 2023 as shown in Table 3.
The Nairobi Securities Exchange (NSE) 20 share index increased from 1,575 points in June 2023
to 1,577 points in July 2023. During the same period, the total number of shares traded increased
by 97.6 per cent from 211 million to 417 million. The number of equity transactions slightly de-
creased from 23,662 in June to 23,569 in July 2023. Equities Market turnover increased by 86.7
per cent from KSh 4.08 billion in June 2023 to KSh 7.62 billion in July 2023, as presented in Ta-
ble 4.
Table 5(a) presents Money Supply statistics. Broad money supply (M3), a key indicator for mone-
tary policy formulation, decreased from KSh 5,568.05 billion in June 2023 to KSh 5,148.50 billion
in July 2023. The value of Gross Foreign Exchange Reserves increased from KSh 1,859.64 bil-
lion in June 2023 to KSh 1,946.75 billion in July 2023. During the same period, the value of Net
Foreign Exchange Reserves increased from KSh 594.10 billion to KSh 628.93 billion as present-
ed in Table 5(b).
3AGRICULTURE
Data on coffee sales and prices are presented in Table 6. There was a significant drop in total
coffee sold in the month of May. The quantity of produced tea decreased from 57,885.91 MT in
May 2023 to 48,127.89 MT in June 2023.The average auction price for Kenya tea in the month of
June 2023 was lower at KSh 291.87 per Kg against KSh 300.45 per Kg in May 2023, as present-
ed in Table 7.
The quantity of cane deliveries decreased from 436.69 thousand metric tonnes in June 2023 to
395.23 thousand metric tonnes in July 2023,as presented in Table 8.
The average monthly retail market prices for a one Kg of maize decreased from KSh 86.3 in June
2023 to KSh 84.6 in July 2023 as presented in Table 9(b). The average monthly retail market
prices for a one Kg of beans decreased from KSh 181.8 in June 2023 to KSh 174.5 in July 2023
as shown in Table 9(d).
Quantity of coffee exported decreased from 7,078.27 MT in June 2023 to 6,174.90 MT in July
2023. Additionally, its value decreased from KSh 5.0 billion to KSh 4.19 billion over the same pe-
riod as shown in Table 10. The quantity of tea exported decreased from 45,317.56 MT in June
2023 to 39,943.62 MT in July 2023. In addition, its value decreased from KSh 15.45 billion to KSh
12.91 billion over the same period as presented in Table 10.
Statistics on monthly export of fresh horticultural produce are presented in Table 11. The quantity
of Cut Flowers exported increased from 8,931.54 MT in June 2023 to 11,715.32 MT in July 2023.
Additionally, the value of Cut Flowers exported increased from KSh 5,186.30 million to KSh
6,926.53 million during the same period. The quantity of Fruits exported increased from
19,518.58 MT in June 2023 to 22,916.07 MT in July 2023. The value of fruits exported increased
from KSh 3,009.73 million to KSh 3,702.47 million during the same period. The quantity of Vege-
tables exported was 5,254.93 MT, while their value was KSh 1,565.52 million in July 2023.

The value of imports decreased from KSh 210.17 billion in June 2023 to KSh 191.69 billion in
July 2023. During the same period, the value of home use imports decreased from KSh 159.33
billion to KSh 140.53 billion.
In July 2023, Kenya major trading partner countries for exports were Uganda with a value of
KSh 10.27 billion followed by Netherlands with a value of KSh 5.52 billion, as presented in Ta-
ble 13(a).Domestic exports by Broad Economic Category indicated that food and beverages
was the main export category in July 2023 accounting for 43.13 per cent of the domestic ex-
ports. Major origins of imports as presented in Table 14(a) indicates that China was the main
import partner with a value of KSh 40.05 billion followed by United Arabs Emirates with a value
of KSh 32.17 billion in July 2023.Imports by Broad Economic Category indicate that non-food
industrial supplies were the main import category in July 2023 with a share of 33.69 per cent of
the total imports in July 2023.
The total number of mobile money transactions increased from 197.42 million in June 2023 to
202.85 million in July 2023. Similarly, the value of the transactions increased from KSh 643.76
billion to 684.64 billion during the same period. The number of Mobile Money Agents increased
from 328,543 in June 2023 to 330,912 in July 2023. During the same period, total registered
mobile money subscriptions increased from 76.99 million to 77.21 million as presented in Table
14(c), As shown in Table 14(d), the value of imports for telecommunication equipment de-
creased from KSh 1.76 billion in May 2023 to KSh 1.50 billion in June 2023.
5ENERGY AND MANUFACTURING
Monthly electricity statistics are presented in Tables 15(a) and 15(b). Total local electricity gen-
eration increased from 1,035.36 million KWh in June 2023 to 1,115.46 million KWh in July
2023. Total electricity consumption increased from 850.53 million KWh in June 2023 to 881.01
million KWh in July 2023.
The national average domestic retail oil prices of motor gasoline premium, light diesel and ker-
osene was KSh 195.32, KSh 180.42 and KSh 170.25 per litre respectively in July 2023 as
shown in Table 15(e). Charcoal prices averaged KSh 70.95 per Kg while the price of a 13-Kg
cylinder of gas averaged KSh 2,787.83 during the same period. In the international market, the
price of the OPEC crude oil basket increased from US Dollars 75.19 per barrel in June 2023 to
US Dollars 81.06 per barrel in July 2023 as shown in Table 15(f).
Tables 17(a) to 17(f) present various monthly manufacturing statistics. Sugar production de-
creased cent from 34,373 MT in June 2023 to 33,328 MT in July 2023.Production of soft drinks
increased from 48,988 thousand litres in February 2023 to 55,405 thousand litres in March
2023.Production of assembled vehicles increased from 972 units in April 2023 to 1,235 units in
May 2023.Production of Galvanized sheets increased from 18,454 metric tonnes in February
2023 to 19,090 metric tonnes in March 2023.
The quantity of cement produced increased from 769,259 MT in April 2023 to 818,697 MT in
May 2023. Additionally, consumption of cement increased from 742,272 MT to 777,697 MT
during the same period. Milk intake in the formal sector decreased from 73.78 million litres in
May 2023 to 71.69 million litres in June 2023.
6TOURISM AND TRANSPORT
The total number of visitors arriving through Jomo Kenyatta (JKIA) and Moi International Air-
ports (MIA) increased from 132,297 persons in June 2023 to 158,100 persons in July 2023, as
shown in Table 18.
Table 19(a) shows international passenger air traffic departures through Jomo Kenyatta Inter-
national Airport. The number of international departures increased by 8.6 per cent from
230,511 in May 2023 to 250,343 in June 2023.Table 19(b) presents international passenger air
traffic arrivals through Jomo Kenyatta International Airport. The number of passengers in-
creased by 14.1 per cent from 231,021 in May 2023 to 263,534 in June 2023.
Table 19(c) presents the number of newly registered motor vehicles by body type. The number
of newly registered motor vehicles rose by 13.4 per cent from 11,419 in April 2023 to 12,945 in
May 2023.
The number of passengers transported via Standard Gauge Railway (SGR) decreased by 7.2
per cent from 214,050 in May 2023 to 198,564 in June 2023. The drop in passengers resulted
in a 6.9 per cent decline in revenue generated to KSh 218.7 million in June. The volume of car-
go transported by SGR declined slightly by 0.2 per cent while the revenue generated during the
period under review decreased by 11.1 per cent to KSh 1,139.0 million in June as shown in Ta-
ble 19(d).Table 19(e) presents Mombasa Port throughput. The volume of cargo through the
port declined by 2.5 per cent from 2,869.3 thousand tonnes in June to 2,796.5 thousand
tonnnes in July 2023. The number of passengers transported through Metre Gauge Railway
(MGR) declined by 5.4 per cent from 308,044 in May to 291,322 in June 2023. Revenue gener-
ated via MGR dropped marginally from KSh 15.1 million per cent in May to KSh 14.9 million in
June 2023 as shown in Table 19(f).
