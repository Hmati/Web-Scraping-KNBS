iiKEY HIGHLIGHTS
The Monthly Leading Economic Indicators report highlights changes in Consumer Price Indices,
inflation rates, interest rates and exchange rates. In addition, the report details changes in se-
lected indicators of international trade, agriculture, energy, manufacturing, construction, tourism
and transport.
MACROECONOMIC INDICATORS
The Consumer Price Index (CPI) increased by 0.4 per cent from an index of 137.55 in December
2023 to an index of 138.15 in January 2024, as indicated in Table 1(a). The overall inflation rate,
as measured by the Consumer Price Indices, increased from 6.6 per cent in December 2023, to
6.9 per cent in January 2024, as presented in Table 1(b).
Table 2 presents the mean monthly foreign exchange rates of the Kenyan Shilling against major
selected trading currencies. In January 2024, the Kenyan Shilling depreciated against all major
selected trading currencies. The average yield rate for 91-day Treasury Bills, which is a bench-
mark for interest rate trends, increased from 15.70 per cent in December 2023 to 16.14 per cent
in January 2024. The Central Bank of Kenya maintained its benchmark interest rate at 12.50 per
cent in January 2024. Inter-Bank Rates rose from 11.65 per cent in December 2023 to 13.65 per
cent in January 2024, as presented in Table 3.
The Nairobi Securities Exchange (NSE) 20 share index grew from 1,501 points in December 2023
to 1,509 points in January 2024. The total number of shares traded dropped from 208 million to
152 million during the same period. The number of Equity transactions increased from 15,329 in
December 2023 to 19,180 in January 2024. Equities Market turnover declined to KSh 2.7 billion
in January 2024, from KSh 3.3 billion recorded in December 2023, as outlined in Table 4.
Tables 5(a) and 5(b) provides statistics on Money Supply. Broad money supply (M3), a key indi-
cator for monetary policy formulation, grew from KSh 5,952.3 billion in November 2023 to KSh
6,039.7 billion in December 2023. Foreign currency deposits increased from KSh 1,497.8 billion
in November 2023 to KSh 1,548.2 billion in December 2023.The value of Gross Foreign Exchange
Reserves rose from KSh 2,180.2 billion in November 2023 to KSh 2,213.5 billion in December
2023. Additionally, the value of Net Foreign Exchange Liabilities rose from KSh 774.7 billion to
KSh 796.5 billion during the same period.
3AGRICULTURE
The quantity of coffee sold increased from 154.2 MT in August 2023 to 339.9 MT in September
2023, as presented in Table 6. Tea production decreased from 52,792.7 MT in October 2023 to
50,905.5 MT in November 2023. The average auction price for Kenya tea in November 2023 was
higher at USD 2.17 per Kg against USD 2.16 per Kg in October 2023. During the eleven-month
period of 2023, the average auction price for Kenya tea stood at 2.24 USD per Kg, as shown in
Table 7.
Cane deliveries more than doubled from 296.2 thousand metric tonnes in November 2023 to
610.0 thousand metric tonnes in December 2023, as presented in Table 8. Tables 9(b) and 9(d)
presents the average retail market prices for one Kg of both maize and beans. The average
monthly retail market price for one Kg of maize decreased KSh 70.2 in December 2023 to KSh
65.9 in January 2024. Similarly, the average monthly retail market price for one Kg of beans de-
clined from KSh 170.3 to KSh 162.3 during the same period.
The quantity of exported coffee increased from 1,478.3 MT in December 2023 to 2,685.8 MT in
January 2024, with a corresponding increase in value from KSh 1.2 billion to KSh 2.1 billion. The
quantity of exported tea increased from 46,524.9 MT in December 2023 to 51,187.6 MT in Janu-
ary 2024. Additionally, its value increased from KSh 17.3 billion to KSh 18.3 billion during the
same period, as outlined in Table 10.
Table 11 provides data on the monthly exports of fresh horticultural produce. The quantity of ex-
ported cut flowers declined from 12,841.9 MT in November 2023 to 4,684.6 MT in December
2023, accompanied by a corresponding decrease in value from KSh 9.5 billion to KSh 3.2 billion.
Similarly, the quantity of exported fruits decreased from 3,474.9 MT in November 2023 to
1,699.5 MT in December 2023, with a drop in value from KSh 0.6 billion to KSh 0.2 billion during
the same period. The quantity of exported vegetables decreased from 14,599.8 MT in November
2023 to 4,777.6 MT in December 2023, with a corresponding decline in value from KSh 7.5 billion
to KSh 1.4 billion.

In January 2024, Uganda and USA emerged as Kenya's major trading partners for exports with
values of KSh 8.6 billion and KSh 7.3 billion respectively, as detailed in Table 13(a). During the
same period, food and beverages comprised 45.3 per cent, while non-food industrial supplies
constituted 25.7 per cent of the total exports, as indicated in Table 13(b).
Table 14(a) outlines the main origins of imports. China was the leading importing partner with a
value of KSh 44.7 billion, followed by India with a value of KSh 28.6 billion in January 2024. The
leading imports category was non-food industrial supplies, accounting for 35.2 per cent of the
total imports in January 2024. Fuel and lubricants constituted 23.1 per cent of the total imports,
while Food and beverages constituted 10.6 per cent of the total imports during the same period,
as presented in Table 14(b).
Table 14(c) highlights mobile money statistics. The number of mobile money agents decreased
from 327,928 in November 2023 to 322,404 in December 2023. The number of mobile money
subscriptions increased from 77.1 million to 77.3 million during the same period. The number of
mobile money transactions rose from 201.3 million in November 2023 to 213.3 million in Decem-
ber 2023. In addition, the total value of transactions rose from KSh 707.6 billion to KSh 788.4
billion during the same period. The value of imports for telecommunication equipment rose from
KSh 3.1 billion in October 2023 to KSh 3.4 billion in November 2023, as shown in Table 14(d).
5ENERGY, CONSTRUCTION AND MANUFACTURING
Tables 15(a) and 15(b) presents
monthly electricity statistics. Lo-
cal electricity generation in-
creased from 970.8 million KWh
in December 2023 to 1,026.5 mil-
lion KWh in January 2024. Total
sales by KPLC rose from 828.3
million KWh in December 2023 to 881.4 million KWh in January 2024. In addition, the total elec-
tricity generation and imports increased from 1,109.5 million KWh to 1,168.7 million KWh during
the same period. Total electricity imports rose from 138.7 million KWh in December 2023 to
142.3 million KWh in January 2024. Conversely, total electricity exports dropped from 4.0 million
KWh to 3.4 million KWh during the same period.
The national average retail prices for motor gasoline premium, light diesel, and illuminating kero-
sene were KSh 207.97, KSh 197.21, and KSh 194.96 per litre, respectively, in January 2024.
Charcoal prices averaged KSh 76.89 per kg, while a 13-kg cylinder of gas averaged KSh
3,055.51, during the same period, as indicated in Table 15(e). On the international market, the
OPEC crude oil basket price increased from US Dollars 79.0 per barrel in December 2023 to US
Dollars 80.04 per barrel in January 2024.
The total value of buildings approved by Nairobi City County rose from KSh 16.2 billion in No-
vember 2023 to KSh 24.9 billion in December 2023. The value of residential buildings approved
increased from KSh 14.0 billion in November 2023 to KSh 21.4 billion in December 2023. Similar-
ly, the value of non-residential buildings approved rose from KSh 2.2 billion to KSh 3.5 billion
during the same period, as outlined in Table 16.
Tables 17(a) to 17(f) outlines various monthly manufacturing statistics. Sugar production in-
creased from 25,179 MT in November 2023 to 48,877 MT in December 2023. Cement production
decreased from 865,310 MT in August 2023 to 831,534 MT in September 2023. Cement con-
sumption decreased from 826,947 MT to 799,297 MT during the same period. Milk intakes in the
formal sector dropped from 75.7 million liters in December 2023 to 74.6 million litres in January
2024.
6TOURISM AND TRANSPORT
The total number of arrivals through
both Jomo Kenyatta (JKIA) and Moi
International Airports (MIA) dropped
from 143,942 persons in December
2023 to 134,665 persons in January
2024, as indicated in Table 18.
Tables 19(a) and 19(b) presents international passengers' air traffic departures and arrivals
through Jomo Kenyatta International Airport. The number of international passenger departures
rose from 282,450 in December 2023 to 282,807 in January 2024. In contrast, international pas-
senger arrivals declined from 306,576 to 269,003 during the same period.
The number of newly registered motor vehicles, classified by body type, declined from 12,217
units in November 2023 to 10,349 units in December 2023. Similarly, the number of newly regis-
tered motorcycles decreased from 3,472 units to 3,223 units over the same period, as indicated
in Table 19(c).
The number of passengers traveling by the Standard Gauge Railway (SGR) increased from
239,411 in November 2023 to 304,605 in December 2023, accompanied by a corresponding reve-
nue increase from KSh 250.5 million to KSh 305.3 million. Cargo volume transported via SGR rose
from 514,843 tonnes to 520,313 tonnes, generating KSh 1.3 billion in revenue during the same
period, as presented in Table 19(d). Metre Gauge Railway (MGR) passenger usage declined from
316,075 in November 2023 to 250,792 in December 2023. However, revenue generated from
MGR passenger traffic rose from KSh 18.6 million to KSh 27.5 million during the same period, as
indicated in Table 19(e).
Table 19(f) presents Mombasa Port throughput. The volume of cargo handled decreased from
3,286.7 thousand tonnes in November 2023 to 3,000.3 thousand tonnes in December 2023. Im-
ports volume decreased from 2,533.8 thousand tonnes in November 2023 to 2,191.4 thousand
tonnes in December 2023. In contrast, the exports volume increased from 367.8 thousand tonnes
to 403.7 thousand tonnes. Transhipment rose by 6.2 per cent while restows dropped by 17.6 per
cent, during the same period.
